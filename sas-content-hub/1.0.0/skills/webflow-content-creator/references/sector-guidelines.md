# Sector-Specific Messaging Guidelines

Use these guidelines to tailor content for each sector's unique challenges, terminology, and priorities.

---

## Local Government

### Audience Profile
- Asset managers in councils and municipalities
- Directors of infrastructure
- Engineers managing community assets
- Finance/budget decision-makers

### Key Challenges
- Limited budgets with ageing infrastructure
- Community expectations vs resource constraints
- Long-term planning with short-term political cycles
- Compliance with state/federal requirements
- Diverse asset types (roads, buildings, parks, drains)

### Relevant Terminology
- Asset management plans (AMPs)
- Long-term financial plans (LTFPs)
- Renewal gap
- Service levels
- Community infrastructure
- Rate-funded assets
- Depreciation
- Fair value

### Content Angles
- Doing more with less
- Community impact of infrastructure decisions
- Compliance without bureaucracy
- Practical approaches for small teams
- Data-driven prioritisation with limited data

### Tone Adjustment
- Acknowledge resource constraints openly
- Focus on practical, achievable improvements
- Emphasise community benefit, not just efficiency
- Avoid assumptions about data maturity

### Example Hook
> "Your council has 400 assets, 2 staff, and a backlog that grows faster than your budget. Here's how to stop fighting fires and start making real progress."

## Water

### Audience Profile
- Asset managers at water utilities
- Operations and maintenance managers
- Reliability engineers
- Network planners
- SCADA/control systems engineers

### Key Challenges
- Critical infrastructure with no downtime tolerance
- Ageing networks (pipes 50-100+ years old)
- Climate resilience (drought, flood, storms)
- Water quality compliance
- Energy costs for pumping
- Remote/distributed assets
- Sensor data overload

### Relevant Terminology
- Pump stations
- Treatment plants
- Trunk mains
- Pressure zones
- SCADA
- NRW (non-revenue water)
- Pipe criticality
- Condition grading
- Bursts/leaks
- EPA compliance

### Content Angles
- Predictive maintenance for pumps
- Pipe failure prediction
- Integrating SCADA with maintenance systems
- Climate adaptation for water infrastructure
- Energy optimisation

### Tone Adjustment
- Acknowledge 24/7 critical nature
- Focus on reliability and continuity
- Emphasise safety and compliance
- Recognise long asset lifecycles

### Example Hook
> "Your pump station has 47 sensors. Your maintenance planner uses 3 of them. Here's how to close that gap without adding complexity."

- Reliability engineers
- Processing plant supervisors
- Asset strategy managers
- Fleet coordinators

### Key Challenges
- Production-driven environment (downtime = lost revenue)
- Harsh operating conditions
- High-value equipment with long lead times
- Remote locations
- Safety-critical systems
- Skills shortage

### Relevant Terminology

- Haul trucks, loaders

- MTBF/MTTR

- Component life tracking

### Content Angles

- Predictive maintenance ROI calculations
- Shutdown optimisation

- Respect operational pressures
- Acknowledge tough conditions

## Universal Principles Across All Sectors

1. **Show sector knowledge** — Use their terminology, understand their challenges
2. **Acknowledge constraints** — Budget, time, skills, data maturity
3. **Lead with outcomes** — What result will they achieve?
4. **Be practical** — Theory is nice, application is essential
5. **Avoid assumptions** — Ask about their specific situation
6. **Respect expertise** — They know their operations; we bring additional perspective
