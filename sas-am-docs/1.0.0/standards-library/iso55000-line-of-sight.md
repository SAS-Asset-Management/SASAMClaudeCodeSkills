# ISO 55000 line of sight — the document hierarchy

The ISO 55000 family defines a "line of sight" from corporate intent to asset activity. The
SAS-AM document suite maps one document to each tier.

```
Organisational Strategic Plan (OSP)   — corporate objectives (client-owned)
        │  translated by
        ▼
SAMP   Strategic Asset Management Plan — how org objectives become AM objectives; policy,
        │                                decision criteria, risk appetite, funding strategy
        │  elaborated into
        ▼
AMP(s) Asset Management Plan(s)        — activities, resources, timescales for an asset class:
        │                                LoS, demand, lifecycle, renewal, risk, financials
        │  operationalised by
        ▼
TMP    Tactical Maintenance Plan       — maintenance tactics & scheduled task list
        ▲  informed by
        │
RCM / FMECA                            — failure-mode analysis selecting the tactics
```

Definitions (ISO 55000:2014 §3):
- **Asset management** (§3.3.1): coordinated activity of an organisation to realise value
  from assets.
- **SAMP** (§3.3.2): documented information that specifies how organisational objectives are
  converted into asset management objectives, the approach for developing asset management
  plans, and the role of the AM system in supporting achievement of the AM objectives.
- **Asset management plan** (§3.3.3): documented information that specifies the activities,
  resources and timescales required for an individual asset, or grouping, to achieve the
  organisation's asset management objectives.

Key principle: **value, alignment, leadership, assurance** (ISO 55000 §2.4.2). Every tier
must trace upward (to org objectives) and downward (to asset activity) — the "golden thread".
