# LinkedIn Ads Audience Targeting Guide — B2B Asset Management

Best practices for LinkedIn Ads audience targeting in the asset management, maintenance, and reliability engineering space. Designed for SAS-AM's target market across Australian infrastructure sectors.

---

## Audience Building Best Practices

### Layered Targeting Strategy

LinkedIn targeting is most effective when you layer multiple attributes. Build audiences using this hierarchy:

**Layer 1: Industry** (broad filter)
Start with target industries to establish the pool.

**Layer 2: Job Function or Job Title** (role filter)
Narrow to relevant functions: Engineering, Operations, Information Technology, General Management.

**Layer 3: Seniority** (authority filter)
Target the appropriate decision-making level for the campaign objective.

**Layer 4: Company Size** (fit filter)
Filter to organisations large enough to have asset management needs and budgets.

### Recommended Audience Configurations

#### Audience A: Decision-Makers (Top of Funnel — Awareness)

- **Job Function:** Engineering, Operations, Information Technology, General Management
- **Seniority:** Director, VP, C-Suite
- **Company Size:** 1,001+ employees
- **Industries:** Utilities, Mining, Manufacturing, Transport, Government
- **Geography:** Australia
- **Estimated Audience Size:** 15,000–40,000

**Use for:** Brand awareness campaigns, thought leadership content, video views

#### Audience B: Decision-Makers (Bottom of Funnel — Lead Gen)

- **Job Titles:** Head of Asset Management, GM Operations, Chief Engineer, CTO, CIO, Director of Maintenance, Director of Engineering
- **Company Size:** 1,001+ employees
- **Industries:** Utilities, Mining, Manufacturing, Transport, Government
- **Geography:** Australia
- **Estimated Audience Size:** 5,000–15,000

**Use for:** Lead generation campaigns, demo requests, consultation offers

**Note:** Using job titles instead of job function + seniority gives more precision but a smaller audience. For lead gen, precision is more important than reach.

#### Audience C: Technical Influencers (Middle of Funnel — Consideration)

- **Job Titles:** Reliability Engineer, Asset Manager, Maintenance Manager, Operations Manager, Asset Planner, Data Scientist
- **Seniority:** Manager, Senior
- **Company Size:** 201+ employees
- **Industries:** Utilities, Mining, Manufacturing, Transport, Government
- **Geography:** Australia
- **Estimated Audience Size:** 20,000–50,000

**Use for:** Whitepaper downloads, case study promotion, webinar registrations, content engagement

#### Audience D: Retargeting — Website Visitors

- **Source:** LinkedIn Insight Tag
- **Audience:** All website visitors (last 180 days)
- **Exclusions:** Customers, job applicants, careers page visitors
- **Estimated Audience Size:** Dependent on website traffic (minimum 300 members required)

**Use for:** Retargeting campaigns across all funnel stages. These users have already shown intent by visiting the website.

#### Audience E: ABM — Target Account List

- **Source:** Company list upload (from CRM or MBP:b2b-research output)
- **Overlay:** Job Function (Engineering, Operations, IT) + Seniority (Director+)
- **Estimated Audience Size:** Dependent on list size

**Use for:** Account-based marketing campaigns targeting specific organisations identified through MBP:b2b-research.

#### Audience F: Lookalike — High-Value Leads

- **Source:** Matched Audience built from CRM contact list of qualified leads or customers
- **Type:** Lookalike audience
- **Geography:** Australia
- **Estimated Audience Size:** Varies (LinkedIn generates this automatically)

**Use for:** Prospecting to reach new professionals who resemble existing high-value contacts.

## Audience Exclusions

Exclusions are as important as inclusions. Always exclude these segments to avoid wasted spend:

### Standard Exclusions (Apply to All Campaigns)

| Exclusion | Why |
|---|---|
| **Company size: 1–50 employees** | Too small for SAS-AM's consulting services |
| **Seniority: Entry, Training** | Not involved in purchasing decisions |
| **Job Function: Human Resources, Legal, Accounting** | Not relevant to asset management decisions (exception: CFO targeting in budget-focused campaigns) |
| **Existing customers** | Upload customer list and exclude (unless running retention/upsell campaigns) |
| **Competitors** | Upload competitor company list and exclude |
| **Job seekers** | Exclude "Open to Work" members where possible |
| **Students and recent graduates** | Seniority: Unpaid, Training |

### Campaign-Specific Exclusions

| Campaign Type | Additional Exclusions |
|---|---|
| **Lead Gen — Decision-Makers** | Exclude Manager and below seniority |
| **Lead Gen — Influencers** | Exclude Director+ to avoid overlap with decision-maker campaigns |
| **Retargeting** | Exclude users who have already converted (form submission, demo request) |
| **ABM** | Exclude companies not on the target account list |

## Matched Audiences Setup Guide

### Website Retargeting (Insight Tag Required)

**Prerequisites:**
- LinkedIn Insight Tag installed on all website pages
- Minimum 300 matched members in the audience

**Recommended segments:**
1. **All website visitors** (last 180 days) — broadest retargeting pool
2. **Key page visitors** — users who visited pricing, services, or case study pages (higher intent)
3. **Blog readers** — users who visited 2+ blog posts (engaged but not yet converting)
4. **Converters — exclude** — users who submitted a form (exclude from lead gen retargeting)

### CRM Contact List Upload

**Format:** CSV with at least email address. LinkedIn matches on:
- Email address (primary match key)
- First name + last name + company (secondary)
- LinkedIn member ID (if available)

**Expected match rate:** 30–70% depending on data quality and whether contacts use personal or work email on LinkedIn.

**Recommended lists to upload:**
1. **Qualified leads (last 12 months)** — for lookalike audience generation
2. **Current customers** — for exclusion from prospecting campaigns
3. **Competitors' known contacts** — for exclusion
4. **Newsletter subscribers** — for custom targeting or exclusion

### Company Account List Upload

**Format:** CSV with company names. Include LinkedIn Company Page URLs where possible for higher match rates.

**Sources for account lists:**
- CRM account data
- MBP:b2b-research prospect lists
- Industry directories (e.g., AWA member list, AiGroup members)
- Conference attendee company lists

**Recommended lists:**
1. **Target accounts** — companies identified as high-value prospects
2. **Competitor companies** — for exclusion
3. **Customer companies** — for exclusion from prospecting; inclusion in retention campaigns

## Targeting Do's and Don'ts

### Do

- **Layer targeting attributes** — industry + job function + seniority + company size gives precision
- **Use job titles for lead gen** — more precise than job function for bottom-of-funnel campaigns
- **Separate audiences into distinct campaigns** — do not mix decision-makers and influencers in the same campaign
- **Use exclusions aggressively** — every irrelevant impression is wasted spend
- **Upload account lists from MBP:b2b-research** — ABM targeting is LinkedIn's strongest capability
- **Monitor audience overlap** — use LinkedIn's audience overlap tool to check for duplicate targeting across campaigns
- **Refresh Matched Audiences quarterly** — CRM lists and account lists go stale
- **Test lookalike audiences** — build from your highest-quality lead list, not all leads

### Don't

- **Don't target too broadly** — "All Directors in Australia" is too wide. Add industry and function filters
- **Don't use interests or groups as primary targeting** — these are self-reported and unreliable. Use as overlays only
- **Don't ignore exclusions** — excluding irrelevant segments is as important as including relevant ones
- **Don't run the same audience across multiple campaigns without exclusions** — this causes self-competition and inflates CPCs
- **Don't target companies with <200 employees** (for SAS-AM) — the consulting engagement economics rarely work at this scale
- **Don't change audiences and creatives simultaneously** — change one variable at a time so you can attribute performance changes
- **Don't rely solely on LinkedIn's audience expansion** — it broadens targeting beyond your specifications. Disable or use with caution

## Audience Testing Framework

When testing new audience segments, follow this structure:

### Test Setup

1. **Control audience**: Existing best-performing audience (unchanged)
2. **Test audience**: One variable changed (e.g., new industry, new seniority level, new job titles)
3. **Budget**: Allocate 20% of campaign budget to test, 80% to control
4. **Duration**: Run for minimum 2 weeks or until statistically significant sample (50+ clicks minimum per variant)
5. **Success metric**: Define before launch (e.g., CPL, conversion rate, lead quality score)

### Test Ideas for Asset Management B2B

| Test | Control | Variant | Hypothesis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Seniority expansion | Director+ only | Add Manager seniority | Managers may convert at higher rates as they have more operational pain awareness |
| Industry expansion | Utilities + Mining | Add Healthcare | Healthcare facilities management has similar asset management challenges |
| Company size expansion | 1,001+ employees | Add 201–1,000 | Mid-market companies may have fewer resources and greater need for consulting |
| Job title specificity | Job function: Engineering | Specific titles: Reliability Engineer, Asset Manager | Job titles may reduce waste on irrelevant engineering roles |
| ABM vs broad targeting | Industry + function targeting | Account list + function overlay | ABM should deliver higher conversion rates at higher CPL |

## Integration Notes

### MBP:b2b-research Integration

- Prospect lists from MBP:b2b-research can be exported as CSV and uploaded as Company Account Lists for ABM targeting
- Decision-maker names identified in b2b-research dossiers can inform job title targeting refinement
- Industry and company size data from b2b-research validates targeting parameters

### MBP:linkedin-post Integration

- Organic post engagement data can inform which topics resonate with the target audience
- Top-performing organic content themes should be tested as paid ad creative themes
- Company Page followers can be retargeted with paid campaigns

### UTM Taxonomy (shared/utm-taxonomy.md)

All LinkedIn Ads campaigns must use standardised UTM parameters:

| Parameter | Value for LinkedIn Ads |
|---|---|
| `utm_source` | `linkedin` |
| `utm_medium` | `paid_social` |
| `utm_campaign` | `linkedin-{objective}-{audience}-{YYYYMM}` |
| `utm_content` | Creative or ad group identifier |

Examples:
- `utm_campaign=linkedin-leadgen-utilities-202603`
- `utm_campaign=linkedin-brand-mining-202604`
- `utm_content=iso55001-awareness-v2`
