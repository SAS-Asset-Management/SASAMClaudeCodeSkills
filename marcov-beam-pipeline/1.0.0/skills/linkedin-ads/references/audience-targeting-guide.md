## Overview

This guide provides targeting recommendations for LinkedIn Ads campaigns promoting asset management, reliability engineering, and industrial services solutions. It is tailored to the Australian B2B market, where smaller professional populations and concentrated industry clusters require precise targeting to balance reach with relevance.

SAS-AM targets **asset-intensive organisations** that manage physical infrastructure and are looking to improve asset performance through advanced analytics, AI/ML, reliability engineering, and ISO 55001 alignment.

LinkedIn is the primary paid channel for reaching asset management decision-makers because it is the only platform that allows simultaneous targeting by job function, seniority, company size, and industry. This precision comes at a higher cost per click (AUD $5--$15) than other platforms, making audience accuracy essential to avoid wasting spend on irrelevant professionals.

## 3. Audience Sizing Guidelines

### 3.1 Minimum Viable Audience Sizes

| Campaign Type | Minimum Audience | Recommended Audience | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sponsored Content (lead gen)** | 50,000 | 80,000--150,000 | Smaller audiences increase CPCs and limit delivery |
| **Sponsored Content (awareness)** | 100,000 | 200,000+ | Broader audiences needed for reach objectives |
| **Message Ads (InMail)** | 15,000 | 30,000--80,000 | Smaller audiences acceptable; higher engagement per send |
| **Text Ads** | 50,000 | 100,000+ | Low CTR format; needs volume for meaningful results |
| **Video Ads** | 50,000 | 100,000+ | Need volume for view completion analysis |
| **Conversation Ads** | 15,000 | 30,000--80,000 | Similar to Message Ads; smaller audiences viable |
| **ABM (account list)** | 1,000 on list | 5,000--20,000 on list | Layer with seniority to control audience quality |

### 3.2 Audience Size Assessment

| Audience Size | Assessment | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **<300 members** | Too small | LinkedIn will not deliver ads. Broaden targeting immediately |
| **300--1,000** | Minimum viable | Expect very limited delivery and high CPCs. Use only for high-value ABM |
| **1,000--10,000** | Narrow but workable | Good for precise lead gen. Monitor frequency closely |
| **10,000--50,000** | Optimal for B2B | Best balance of precision and delivery for LinkedIn B2B campaigns |
| **50,000--150,000** | Broad but still targeted | Good for awareness campaigns; consider tightening for lead gen |
| **>150,000** | Too broad for B2B | Likely includes irrelevant professionals. Add targeting layers |

### 3.3 Australian Market Considerations

The Australian professional market is significantly smaller than the US or UK markets. This has practical implications for LinkedIn Ads targeting:

| Factor | Impact | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **Total LinkedIn members (AU)** | ~14 million | Audience sizes will be smaller than global benchmarks suggest |
| **B2B decision-makers in asset management** | ~50,000--80,000 (estimated) | Broad audiences may need to combine multiple job functions |
| **Audience overlap** | Multiple campaigns targeting similar audiences will compete with each other | Monitor frequency across campaigns; use audience exclusions |
| **Audience exhaustion** | Smaller audiences fatigue faster | Rotate creative every 4--6 weeks; monitor frequency closely |
| **Niche verticals (e.g. water utilities)** | May fall below minimum audience thresholds | Combine with related industries or broaden seniority levels |
| **Testing cycle duration** | Data accrues more slowly | Allow 2--4 weeks per test for statistically meaningful results |

### 3.4 When Audiences Are Too Small

If the target audience falls below minimum thresholds, consider these strategies (in order of preference):

1. **Broaden seniority** -- add one seniority level up or down (e.g. add "Senior" to a Manager+ audience)
2. **Add related job functions** -- include adjacent functions (e.g. add "Quality Assurance" alongside "Engineering")
3. **Add related industries** -- include adjacent verticals (e.g. add "Construction" alongside "Utilities")
4. **Remove company size filters** -- if already filtering by industry and function, company size may be redundant
5. **Add skills targeting** -- use "member skills" as an AND overlay rather than replacing job function targeting
6. **Combine with New Zealand** -- add NZ to the location targeting to increase audience pool (~15--20% uplift)
7. **Use Message Ads** -- InMail format has lower minimum audience requirements and higher per-member engagement

> **Do not** sacrifice targeting quality solely to hit minimum audience thresholds. A well-targeted audience of 30,000 will outperform a diluted audience of 100,000 on CPL and lead quality metrics.

## 4. Exclusion Recommendations

### 4.1 Job Titles to Exclude

Exclude these job titles to reduce wasted spend on irrelevant impressions and low-quality leads:

| Exclusion Category | Job Titles to Exclude | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Students & interns** | Intern, Graduate, Trainee, Apprentice, Student | Not decision-makers; inflates lead volume with unqualified contacts |
| **Individual contributors (junior)** | Technician, Operator, Trade Assistant, Labourer | Typically not involved in purchasing decisions |
| **Sales & marketing** | Sales Representative, Account Executive, Marketing Coordinator, BDM | Likely competitors or vendors; not target buyers |
| **HR & recruitment** | HR Manager, Recruiter, Talent Acquisition | Not relevant to asset management solutions |
| **Academic & research** | Professor, Lecturer, Researcher, PhD Candidate | Rarely have purchasing authority for enterprise software |
| **Consultants (generic)** | Consultant, Freelancer, Independent Contractor | May be competitors; quality varies significantly |
| **Job seekers** | Any member flagged as "Open to Work" | Not in a position to make purchasing decisions |

### 4.3 Company Size Exclusions

|---|---|---|

### 4.4 Seniority Exclusions

| Seniority to Exclude | When to Exclude | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | Always | Not involved in purchasing decisions |

### 4.5 Other Standard Exclusions

| Exclusion Type | Detail | How to Implement |
|---|---|---|

| **Existing customers** | Exclude current customers from acquisition campaigns | Upload CRM customer list as exclusion; still target for upsell/retention campaigns |
| **Recent converters** | Exclude members who have already submitted a Lead Gen Form | Use Lead Gen Form retargeting audience as exclusion |
| **Geographic exclusions** | Exclude locations outside service areas | Remove regions where you cannot service clients |
| **Job functions: HR, Legal, Accounting** | Not relevant to asset management decisions | Apply as function exclusion (exception: CFO for budget-focused content) |

### 4.6 Campaign-Specific Exclusion Rules

| Campaign Type | Additional Exclusions |
|---|---|

## 5. Matched Audience Strategies

### 5.1 Website Retargeting

**Prerequisite:** LinkedIn Insight Tag must be installed on all website pages.

| Retargeting Audience | Definition | Recommended Use | Minimum Size |
|---|---|---|---|
| **All website visitors** | Anyone who visited any page in the last 90--180 days | Broad retargeting; brand reinforcement | 300 (1,000+ recommended) |
| **Key page visitors** | Visitors to pricing, demo, services, or contact pages | High-intent retargeting; conversion campaigns | 300 |
| **Content consumers** | Visitors to blog, resources, case study, or whitepaper pages | Consideration-stage nurturing | 300 |
| **Visited but not converted** | Website visitors minus converters | Re-engagement with stronger CTA or offer | 300 |
| **Blog readers (2+ posts)** | Users who visited two or more blog posts | Engaged but not yet converting; serve deeper content | 300 |
| **Converters (exclude)** | Users who submitted a form | Exclude from lead gen retargeting to avoid wasted spend | No minimum |

**Best practices for retargeting in Australia:**

- Website retargeting audiences in Australia are typically smaller than in larger markets. Expect audiences of 1,000--10,000 for niche B2B websites.
- Use 90-day or 180-day lookback windows to build sufficient audience size (default 30 days may be too small).
- Layer retargeting with job function or seniority filters to ensure you are retargeting relevant professionals, not all website visitors.
- Serve different creative to retargeting audiences -- do not show the same awareness content they have already seen.
- Exclude careers page visitors and job applicants from retargeting audiences.

### 5.2 CRM Contact List Upload

Upload contact lists from your CRM to create Matched Audiences for targeted campaigns.

| List Type | Purpose | Minimum List Size | Match Rate (Expected) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Open opportunities** | Accelerate deals in pipeline with targeted ads | 300 contacts | 60--80% | Decision-makers see reinforcing content during evaluation |
| **MQLs in nurture** | Move marketing-qualified leads toward sales readiness | 300 contacts | 60--80% | Serve case studies, testimonials, bottom-of-funnel content |
| **Lapsed leads** | Re-engage leads that went cold | 300 contacts | 50--70% | Lower match rate due to job changes; refresh lists regularly |
| **Event attendees** | Retarget webinar or conference attendees | 300 contacts | 60--80% | Follow up with related content, post-event offers |
| **Newsletter subscribers** | Deepen engagement with known contacts | 1,000 contacts | 60--80% | Supplement email with LinkedIn touchpoints |
| **Qualified leads (last 12 months)** | Seed audience for lookalike generation | 300 contacts | 60--80% | Use highest-quality leads for best lookalike results |
| **Exclusion: existing customers** | Prevent acquisition ads reaching current clients | No minimum | 60--80% | Upload customer list and apply as exclusion audience |
| **Exclusion: competitors** | Prevent ads reaching competitor employees | No minimum | 60--80% | Upload known competitor contacts |

**Tips for CRM uploads:**

- Use work email addresses for highest match rates. Personal email addresses (Gmail, Hotmail) will not match.
- Include first name, last name, company name, and job title alongside email for improved matching.
- Refresh uploaded lists monthly to account for job changes and new contacts.
- Segment lists by lifecycle stage for differentiated messaging.
- LinkedIn matches on email address (primary), then first name + last name + company (secondary), then LinkedIn member ID (if available).
- Expected match rate for Australian B2B CRM data: 30--70% depending on data quality and whether contacts use personal or work email on LinkedIn.

### 5.3 Account List Upload (ABM)

Upload company account lists to target all professionals at specific organisations.

| List Type | Purpose | Recommended Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 target accounts** | High-value ABM targets for dedicated campaigns | 50--500 companies | Layer with seniority (Director+) for decision-maker focus |
| **Tier 2 target accounts** | Broader ABM list for awareness and engagement | 200--2,000 companies | Wider seniority range acceptable |
| **Industry watchlist** | Companies in a specific vertical for industry campaigns | 100--1,000 companies | Useful for vertical-specific content |
| **Competitor customers** | Employees at competitor client companies | 50--500 companies | Conquest campaigns; use with caution |
| **Customer companies (exclude)** | Prevent prospecting ads reaching existing clients | No minimum | Exclude from acquisition; include in retention |
| **Competitor companies (exclude)** | Prevent ads reaching competitor employees | No minimum | Always exclude |

**ABM targeting tips for the Australian market:**

- Australian account lists are typically smaller than global ABM programmes. A Tier 1 list of 50--100 companies is common and viable.
- Layer account lists with job function + seniority to avoid serving ads to every employee at target companies (e.g. receptionists, interns).
- Monitor account-level engagement metrics to identify which target companies are responding.
- Align account lists with MBP:b2b-research prospect dossiers for consistent targeting across paid and outbound channels.
- Include LinkedIn Company Page URLs in the upload CSV where possible for higher match rates.

**Sources for account lists:**

- CRM account data
- MBP:b2b-research prospect lists
- Industry directories (e.g. AWA member list, AiGroup members)
- Conference attendee company lists
- Industry body membership directories

### 5.4 Lookalike Audiences

Create lookalike audiences based on high-performing seed audiences to find new prospects with similar characteristics.

| Seed Audience | Recommended For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **High-quality leads (A-grade)** | Finding more prospects matching your ICP | Best seed for lead quality; requires sufficient A-grade lead volume |
| **Converters (all Lead Gen Form submissions)** | Expanding lead volume | Broader than A-grade only; may dilute quality |
| **Website visitors (key pages)** | Prospecting at consideration stage | Requires Insight Tag and sufficient visitor volume |
| **CRM contacts (open opportunities)** | Finding prospects similar to those already in pipeline | Strong intent signal as seed |
| **Account list (Tier 1 customers)** | Finding companies similar to best customers | Company-level lookalike; useful for ABM expansion |

**Lookalike audience best practices:**

- Seed audiences must have at least 300 members for LinkedIn to generate a lookalike.
- Lookalike audiences in Australia will be smaller than in larger markets. Expect 50,000--200,000 members depending on the seed.
- Always layer lookalike audiences with at least one additional targeting criterion (e.g. industry, seniority) to maintain relevance.
- Monitor lead quality closely -- lookalikes may introduce audience segments that look similar on paper but have different purchase intent.
- A/B test lookalike audiences against your manual targeting to compare CPL and lead quality.
- Build lookalike audiences from your highest-quality leads, not all leads.

## 6. Australian B2B Targeting Tips

### 6.1 Market Size Realities

Australia has approximately 14 million LinkedIn members. The B2B professional population relevant to asset management and industrial services is estimated at 50,000--80,000 decision-makers nationally. This is roughly 5--10% of the equivalent US audience.

**Implications:**

- Audiences that work at 200,000+ in the US may yield only 15,000--30,000 in Australia.
- Frequency management is critical -- members see your ads more often in smaller audience pools.
- Budget efficiency is more important than in larger markets. Every dollar of wasted spend on irrelevant impressions has a proportionally larger impact.
- Testing cycles take longer because data accrues more slowly. Allow 2--4 weeks per test to accumulate statistically meaningful results.

### 6.2 State-Level Targeting

For campaigns targeting specific regions or states, consider these industry concentrations:

| State / Territory | Key Industries (Asset Management) | Major Employers / Clusters | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Western Australia** | Mining, Oil & Gas, Utilities | BHP, Rio Tinto, Woodside, Fortescue, Synergy, Water Corporation | Largest concentration of mining asset management professionals |
| **Queensland** | Mining, Utilities, Government | Glencore, Stanwell, CS Energy, Ergon Energy, TMR | Mining (central/north QLD) and government (Brisbane) |
| **New South Wales** | Utilities, Transport, Government, Manufacturing | Sydney Water, Transport for NSW, Ausgrid, AGL | Largest total professional population; diverse industries |
| **Victoria** | Utilities, Manufacturing, Transport | Melbourne Water, Metro Trains, AusNet Services | Strong manufacturing and utility presence |
| **South Australia** | Defence, Utilities, Mining | ASC, SA Water, BHP Olympic Dam, Santos | Defence and utilities hub |
| **Northern Territory** | Mining, Government, Utilities | INPEX, Power and Water Corporation, NT Government | Small but concentrated; remote asset management |
| **Tasmania** | Utilities, Government, Forestry | Hydro Tasmania, TasNetworks, TasPorts | Small market; combine with national campaigns |
| **ACT** | Government | Commonwealth Government departments | Government-focused campaigns only |

**State-level targeting recommendations:**

- Use state-level targeting for localised campaigns (e.g. a WA mining-focused campaign or a Sydney utilities event).
- Combine smaller states (TAS, NT, ACT) with national campaigns to avoid audience sizes falling below minimum thresholds.
- For national campaigns, do not apply state-level targeting -- let LinkedIn optimise delivery across all of Australia.
- Monitor geographic performance in campaign reports (Phase 2 of the analysis) to identify state-level variations in engagement and lead quality.

### 6.3 Language and Localisation

- Target English-language profiles only (default for Australia).
- Use Australian English in all ad copy (e.g. "optimise" not "optimize", "organisation" not "organization").
- Reference Australian standards (e.g. ISO 55000 as adopted by Standards Australia) and local regulatory bodies (e.g. AMCA, MESA).
- Avoid US-centric terminology (e.g. use "maintenance programme" not "maintenance program" in non-software contexts).
- Reference Australian-specific regulations and compliance requirements where relevant.

### 6.4 Seasonal Considerations

| Period | Impact on LinkedIn Ads | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **January** | Low activity; professionals returning from summer holidays | Reduce spend or run awareness-only campaigns in early January; ramp up from mid-January |
| **February--March** | Budget cycles begin; strong engagement | Peak lead generation period; increase spend |
| **April** | Easter break; brief dip | Pause or reduce spend over Easter week |
| **May--June** | End of financial year (EOFY) | Decision-makers spending remaining budget; strong for late-funnel offers |
| **July** | New financial year; budget setting | Good for awareness; decision-makers planning new initiatives |
| **August--October** | Strong B2B engagement period | Peak lead generation period; increase spend |
| **November** | Conference season; pre-holiday wind-down | Good for event promotion; engagement may dip late November |
| **December** | Holiday period; low engagement | Reduce spend significantly; awareness-only if active |

### 6.5 Cross-Tasman (New Zealand) Expansion

When Australian audiences are too small, adding New Zealand can increase audience size by approximately 15--20%.

| Consideration | Detail |
|---|---|
| **NZ LinkedIn members** | ~2.5 million |
| **Industry alignment** | Utilities, government, dairy/agriculture (different from AU mining focus) |
| **Currency** | NZD -- ensure campaign budgets account for currency differences |
| **Regulatory differences** | Different standards bodies; some regulatory overlap (e.g. joint ANZSIC codes) |
| **When to include NZ** | When AU audience is below minimum threshold; when the product/service is available in NZ; for awareness campaigns |
| **When to exclude NZ** | When the offer is AU-only; when follow-up capability does not extend to NZ; for highly localised campaigns |

| **Lead Gen -- Bottom of Funnel** | Decision-makers (2.7) | Job title targeting | 5,000 | Non-decision-maker titles |
| **Brand Awareness** | Executive audience (2.3) | C-Suite/VP + broad industries | 100,000 | Entry-level, non-target functions |
| **ABM -- Tier 1** | Account list (2.9) + seniority | Account list + Director/VP/C-Suite | 1,000 (on list) | Non-decision-makers at target companies |
| **Retargeting** | Website visitors (2.8) | Retargeting + job function filter | 300 | Existing customers (for acquisition), recent converters |
| **Nurture** | CRM upload (5.2) | MQL list + consideration content | 300 | Closed-lost (>12 months), disqualified |
| **Event Promotion** | Core audience + event topic match | Job function + seniority + skills | 30,000 | Exclude registrants |

### 7.2 Targeting Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**

- Layer at least two targeting dimensions (e.g. job function + seniority) to maintain relevance
- Use exclusions actively to prevent wasted spend
- Monitor audience size after applying targeting layers -- ensure it remains above minimum thresholds
- Refresh Matched Audiences (CRM uploads, account lists) monthly
- Test different audience combinations in parallel with consistent creative to isolate audience impact
- Track lead quality by audience segment, not just volume and CPL
- Align targeting with the personas defined in Section 1
- Separate audiences into distinct campaigns -- do not mix decision-makers and influencers in the same campaign
- Upload account lists from MBP:b2b-research for ABM targeting
- Use LinkedIn's audience overlap tool to check for duplicate targeting across campaigns

**Don't:**

- Target by job title alone -- too narrow and excludes equivalent titles (except for bottom-of-funnel lead gen)
- Use "OR" logic across unrelated targeting dimensions (e.g. "Engineers OR Marketing Managers")
- Apply all targeting layers simultaneously -- over-targeting will shrink audiences below viable thresholds
- Ignore frequency -- in the Australian market, small audiences lead to rapid frequency build-up
- Assume global benchmarks apply -- Australian audiences are smaller, more expensive, and require adjusted expectations
- Run the same audience across multiple campaigns without exclusions -- you will compete against yourself in the auction
- Neglect suppression lists -- always exclude existing customers from acquisition campaigns and recent converters from active lead gen
- Use interests or groups as primary targeting -- these are self-reported and unreliable; use as overlays only
- Rely solely on LinkedIn's audience expansion -- it broadens targeting beyond your specifications; disable or use with caution
- Change audiences and creatives simultaneously -- change one variable at a time so you can attribute performance changes

## 8. Audience Testing Framework

### 8.1 A/B Testing Structure

When testing new audience segments, follow this framework:

| Element | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| **Test structure** | One variable at a time (e.g. test seniority A vs seniority B with same creative) |
| **Control audience** | Existing best-performing audience (unchanged) |
| **Test audience** | One variable changed (e.g. new industry, new seniority level, new job titles) |
| **Budget split** | 80% to control, 20% to test |
| **Minimum budget per variant** | AUD $500--$1,000 over 2--4 weeks |
| **Minimum conversions per variant** | 15--20 conversions for statistical confidence (50+ clicks minimum) |
| **Primary metric** | Cost per Qualified Lead (CPQL), not just CPL |
| **Secondary metrics** | Lead quality ratio (A+B %), conversion rate, engagement rate |
| **Test duration** | Minimum 14 days; 21--28 days preferred for Australian B2B |

### 8.2 Suggested Tests for SAS-AM

| Test # | Hypothesis | Audience A (Control) | Audience B (Variant) | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Job function targeting outperforms job title targeting | Job functions (Operations + Engineering) | Job titles (Asset Manager, Reliability Engineer, etc.) | CPQL |
| 2 | Director+ seniority delivers higher-quality leads | Manager + Director + VP + C-Suite | Director + VP + C-Suite only | Lead quality ratio |
| 3 | Utilities vertical outperforms cross-industry | Utilities industry only | Utilities + Mining + Manufacturing | CPQL and volume |
| 4 | Lookalike audiences find viable new prospects | Manual targeting (core audience) | Lookalike from A-grade leads | CPQL and volume |
| 5 | Retargeting converts at lower CPL than prospecting | Prospecting (core audience) | Website retargeting audience | CPL and conversion rate |
| 6 | Account list ABM outperforms broad targeting | Broad industry targeting | Tier 1 account list + seniority overlay | CPQL and account engagement |
| 7 | Mid-market expansion is viable | Company size 1,001+ only | Add 201--1,000 company size | CPQL and volume |
| 8 | Healthcare vertical is a viable expansion | Core industries only | Add Healthcare (facilities management) | CPQL and lead relevance |

- Top-performing organic content themes should be tested as paid ad creative themes.
- Company Page followers can be retargeted with paid campaigns.
