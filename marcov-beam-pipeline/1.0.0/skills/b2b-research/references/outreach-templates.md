## Email 1: Problem-Led Initial Outreach (Day 1)

**Goal**: Earn the right to a brief conversation to validate whether they have the problem you hypothesise.

**Subject**: {{PROBLEM_FOCUSED_SUBJECT_LINE}}

---

Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

{{OPENING_LINE_REFERENCING_THEIR_SITUATION — not your offering}}

I noticed {{SPECIFIC_EVIDENCE_OF_POTENTIAL_PROBLEM}}. If I'm reading this correctly, you may be experiencing {{ARTICULATED_PROBLEM_HYPOTHESIS}}.

If that's off base, ignore this — but if it resonates, I'd welcome 15 minutes to understand whether it's a priority you're actively addressing or something that's been on the backburner.

Not selling anything today — genuinely curious whether what I'm seeing from the outside matches reality on the inside.

Best regards,
{{YOUR_NAME}}
{{YOUR_TITLE}} | {{YOUR_COMPANY}}
{{YOUR_PHONE}} | {{YOUR_EMAIL}}

---

**What makes this work**:
- Leads with their world, not your offering
- Demonstrates you've done research (specific evidence)
- Offers an easy out ("if I'm off base")
- Sets a low-commitment ask (understand, not buy)
- Explicitly names this isn't a sales call

**Goal**: Provide genuine value regardless of whether they engage. Build credibility.

**Subject**: Re: {{ORIGINAL_SUBJECT}} — something relevant to {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}}

---

Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

Following up briefly — not to chase, but to share something relevant.

{{GENUINE_VALUE_ADD — article, benchmark, insight, or data point relevant to their problem domain}}

This reminded me of our conversation thread. If {{PROBLEM_HYPOTHESIS}} is something you're actively thinking about, I'd still welcome a quick chat. If not, at least this might be useful.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}

---

**What makes this work**:
- Gives before asking
- Value is useful regardless of whether they engage
- Keeps the door open without pressure
- Demonstrates domain expertise through what you share

## Email 3: Permission-Based Close (Day 14)

**Goal**: Give them explicit permission to disengage. Respect earns respect.

**Subject**: Closing the loop — {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}} at {{COMPANY_NAME}}

---

Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

I'll keep this brief. I reached out about {{PROBLEM_HYPOTHESIS}} — if that's not a priority right now, totally understood.

If it is something you're working on:
{{ONE_SENTENCE_VALUE_STATEMENT_TIED_TO_THEIR_PROBLEM}}

Either way, I'll keep an eye out for anything useful to share. No response needed if the timing isn't right.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}

---

**What makes this work**:
- Explicitly closes the loop
- No guilt, no pressure, no "just following up"
- One last clear value statement
- Permission to not respond builds trust for future

## Phone Call Script (Day 10)

**Goal**: Validate problem hypothesis, not pitch solution.

**Opening**:
"Hi {{FIRST_NAME}}, this is {{YOUR_NAME}}. I sent an email last week about {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}}. Have you got two minutes?"

**If yes**:
"I noticed {{SPECIFIC_EVIDENCE}} and was curious — is {{PROBLEM_HYPOTHESIS}} something that's actively on your radar, or is it background noise right now?"

**Listen first. Then**:
"That's helpful to understand. [Respond to what they actually said.] Would it be useful to have a longer conversation about how other organisations are tackling this?"

**Bridge to diagnostic**:
"I'm not looking to pitch anything — I'm genuinely trying to understand whether what I'm seeing from the outside matches reality. If it does, maybe we can help. If not, at least we've both learned something."

**If no / voicemail**:
"No worries — I'll send a quick follow-up email. Thanks, {{FIRST_NAME}}."

- {{OPEN_QUESTIONS_FOR_NEXT_CONVERSATION}}

{{YOUR_NAME}}
{{YOUR_TITLE}} | {{YOUR_COMPANY}}

- Next step is about understanding, not selling

## Disqualification Email

**Use when research suggests the prospect is NOT a fit — don't force it.**

**Subject**: Honest assessment — may not be the right fit

---

Hi {{FIRST_NAME}},

I've been looking into {{PROBLEM_DOMAIN}} at {{COMPANY_NAME}}, and I want to be honest: I'm not sure we're the right fit right now.

{{REASON_FOR_DISQUALIFICATION — be specific and respectful}}

If that changes or I've misjudged the situation, I'd be happy to revisit. For now, I'll step back rather than waste your time.

Best,
{{YOUR_NAME}}

---

**What makes this work**:
- Respects their time by not pursuing a bad fit
- Demonstrates integrity (they'll remember this)
- Leaves door open without being pushy
- Qualifying out is a success, not a failure

## Template Selection Guide

| Situation | Template to Use |
|-----------|-----------------|
| First contact — cold | Email 1: Problem-Led Initial Outreach |
| No response after first email | Email 2: Value-First Follow-Up |
| Still no response | Email 3: Permission-Based Close |
| After discovery conversation | Post-Discovery Follow-Up |
| Research shows poor fit | Disqualification Email |
| Building network connection | LinkedIn Connection Request |
| Phone follow-up | Phone Call Script |

---

## Tone Guidelines (BEAM-Aligned)

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Lead with their problem | Lead with your solution |
| Use specific evidence from research | Use generic industry statements |
| Ask questions to validate | Assume you know their situation |
| Offer genuine value | Use value as bait for a call |
| Give permission to disengage | Pressure for a response |
| Qualify out if no fit | Force-fit every prospect |
| Confirm understanding | Rush to propose |
