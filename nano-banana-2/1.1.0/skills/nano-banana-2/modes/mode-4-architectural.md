# Mode 4 — Architectural

## When to use

Infrastructure and sector-positioning posts — "who we serve", capability statements, sector landing pages — where mood and gravitas matter more than explanation.

## Template

Insert the brand frame from `SKILL.md` verbatim at `[FIXED BRAND FRAME]`.

```
Create a sophisticated, minimal illustration of [SIMPLIFIED REAL INFRASTRUCTURE FORM], reduced to clean architectural forms. [COMPOSITION DIRECTIVE]. Spare and considered, with strong simple geometry and generous negative space. A single element or edge picked out in the green accent.

[FIXED BRAND FRAME]

Format: [ASPECT]. Strong compositional line, with [OPEN-SPACE REGION] left as open uncluttered space for adding text later.
```

## Slots

- **SIMPLIFIED REAL INFRASTRUCTURE FORM** — the real piece of infrastructure (viaduct, port crane, water reservoir, transmission tower, road overpass), reduced to clean architectural forms.
- **COMPOSITION DIRECTIVE** — one sentence describing the compositional sweep and where the green accent lands.
- **ASPECT** — `a wide 16:9 landscape composition` (default), `a square 1:1 composition`, or `a tall 4:5 portrait composition`.
- **OPEN-SPACE REGION** — e.g. the upper-left quadrant, the right third, the lower band.

## Worked example — rail sector-positioning post

- **SIMPLIFIED REAL INFRASTRUCTURE FORM:** an elevated rail viaduct curving away into the distance.
- **COMPOSITION DIRECTIVE:** the viaduct sweeping from lower left toward an upper-right vanishing point, clean repeating arches, calm and confident, one arch subtly accented in green.
- **ASPECT:** a wide 16:9 landscape composition.
- **OPEN-SPACE REGION:** the upper-left region above the viaduct line.

Assembled prompt:

```
Create a sophisticated, minimal illustration of an elevated rail viaduct curving away into the distance, reduced to clean architectural forms. The viaduct sweeping from lower left toward an upper-right vanishing point, clean repeating arches, calm and confident, one arch subtly accented in green. Spare and considered, with strong simple geometry and generous negative space. A single element or edge picked out in the green accent.

Visual style: clean, sophisticated, intentional — the look of a high-end consultancy report or quality editorial publication. Flat with crisp clean edges; no outer glow, no halo, no soft luminous bloom, no glassy or frosted texture, no glossy 3D render. Subtle, restrained, confident.

Colour: a deep navy blue (hex #002244) dominates as background and base. A bright lime-green (hex #69BE28) appears ONLY as a precise, deliberate accent — on a single highlighted element, a callout, or a key line. It must never appear as a glow, haze, gradient field, starburst or background light. Support these with cool greys and a soft off-white. Use no other colours.

Lighting: soft, even, calm and assured.

Do not include any text, words, letters, numbers, logos, people or faces. Avoid clutter, neon, rainbow palettes, lens flares, and a generic stock-image look.

Format: a wide 16:9 landscape composition. Strong compositional line, with the upper-left region above the viaduct line left as open uncluttered space for adding text later.
```
