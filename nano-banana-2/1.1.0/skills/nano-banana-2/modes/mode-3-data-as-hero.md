# Mode 3 — Data as Hero

## When to use

Analytical posts — maturity assessments, Weibull / reliability analysis, fleet simulation, analytics results, where a chart or numerical artefact is the point.

## Template

Insert the brand frame from `SKILL.md` verbatim at `[FIXED BRAND FRAME]`.

```
Create a clean, elegant data visualisation in the style of a high-quality analytical report, showing [CHART TYPE]. [COMPOSITION DIRECTIVE]. The chart is precise and well-composed, the visual language of serious quantitative analysis. Plotted lines and forms in cool greys and off-white, with the key series, peak or result highlighted in the green accent.

[FIXED BRAND FRAME]

Format: [ASPECT]. The chart as the clear focal point, with [OPEN-SPACE REGION] left as open uncluttered space for adding text later.
```

## Slots

- **CHART TYPE** — the specific chart form (radar/spider chart, Weibull plot, survival curve, bathtub curve, scatter plot, bar chart, etc).
- **COMPOSITION DIRECTIVE** — one sentence describing what the chart shows, the shape of the data, and where the green accent lands.
- **ASPECT** — `a wide 16:9 landscape composition` (default), `a square 1:1 composition`, or `a tall 4:5 portrait composition`.
- **OPEN-SPACE REGION** — e.g. the upper-left quadrant, the right third, the lower band.

## Worked example — maturity assessment post

- **CHART TYPE:** a radar / spider maturity chart with several axes.
- **COMPOSITION DIRECTIVE:** a single maturity profile plotted across eight or nine axes, the shape uneven to suggest real assessment findings, one weaker axis subtly emphasised in green to imply a priority area.
- **ASPECT:** a wide 16:9 landscape composition.
- **OPEN-SPACE REGION:** the upper-left region.

Assembled prompt:

```
Create a clean, elegant data visualisation in the style of a high-quality analytical report, showing a radar / spider maturity chart with several axes. A single maturity profile plotted across eight or nine axes, the shape uneven to suggest real assessment findings, one weaker axis subtly emphasised in green to imply a priority area. The chart is precise and well-composed, the visual language of serious quantitative analysis. Plotted lines and forms in cool greys and off-white, with the key series, peak or result highlighted in the green accent.

Visual style: clean, sophisticated, intentional — the look of a high-end consultancy report or quality editorial publication. Flat with crisp clean edges; no outer glow, no halo, no soft luminous bloom, no glassy or frosted texture, no glossy 3D render. Subtle, restrained, confident.

Colour: a deep navy blue (hex #002244) dominates as background and base. A bright lime-green (hex #69BE28) appears ONLY as a precise, deliberate accent — on a single highlighted element, a callout, or a key line. It must never appear as a glow, haze, gradient field, starburst or background light. Support these with cool greys and a soft off-white. Use no other colours.

Lighting: soft, even, calm and assured.

Do not include any text, words, letters, numbers, logos, people or faces. Avoid clutter, neon, rainbow palettes, lens flares, and a generic stock-image look.

Format: a wide 16:9 landscape composition. The chart as the clear focal point, with the upper-left region left as open uncluttered space for adding text later.
```
