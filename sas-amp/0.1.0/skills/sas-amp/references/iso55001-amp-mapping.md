## Clause Summaries

### Clause 4 — Context of the Organisation

The organisation must understand its internal and external context, stakeholder needs, and define the scope of its AM system. A decision-making framework must be established that defines how value is determined and what criteria guide AM decisions. The framework must consider the organisation's context, stakeholder requirements, scope, and risks/opportunities.

**AMP demonstration:** The Introduction section establishes organisational context, scope, and stakeholder landscape. The Levels of Service section translates stakeholder needs into measurable targets.

### Clause 5 — Leadership

Top management must demonstrate commitment by ensuring the AM policy, SAMP, and objectives are compatible with strategic direction. They must ensure integration into business processes, resource availability, and promote continual improvement.

**AMP demonstration:** The Introduction section documents organisational commitment, strategic linkages, and policy alignment. The AMP itself is a tangible demonstration of leadership commitment.

### Clause 6 — Planning

The organisation must address risks and opportunities through systematic processes. It must develop a SAMP containing strategies, objectives, and approaches. AM objectives must be measurable, aligned with organisational objectives, and monitored. Plans to achieve objectives must specify what, who, when, how, and at what cost.

**AMP demonstration:** The Risk Management section addresses Clause 6.1. The Levels of Service and Financial Summary sections address 6.2. Every section of the AMP contributes to 6.2.3 by specifying actions, resources, responsibilities, and timeframes.

### Clause 7 — Support

Resources must be determined and provided. Personnel competence must be ensured through education, training, and assessment. Awareness of the AM policy and its implications must be fostered. Communication needs must be determined. Data and information must be specified, collected, and managed to support decision-making. Knowledge must be retained and developed.

**AMP demonstration:** The Financial Summary addresses resources (7.1). AM Practices addresses competence (7.2), awareness (7.3), communication (7.4), and knowledge (7.7). Data requirements are embedded throughout the plan.

### Clause 8 — Operation

Lifecycle processes must be planned and controlled, including creation, acquisition, utilisation, maintenance, improvement, renewal, and disposal. Changes must be controlled and externally provided services must meet requirements.

**AMP demonstration:** The Asset Lifecycle Management section is the primary response to Clause 8, covering maintenance plans, renewal strategies, creation/acquisition, and disposal across the full asset lifecycle.

### Clause 9 — Performance Evaluation

The organisation must determine what to monitor and measure, the methods to use, and when to analyse and evaluate results. Internal audits must be conducted at planned intervals. Management reviews must consider the status of previous actions, changes in context, and performance information.

**AMP demonstration:** The Improvement and Monitoring section addresses all three subclauses through performance measures, audit provisions, and review processes.

### Clause 10 — Improvement

Continual improvement must be pursued. Nonconformities must be addressed through corrective action. Predictive actions must be established to identify potential issues before they occur.

**AMP demonstration:** The Improvement and Monitoring section details the improvement program, corrective action processes, and predictive/preventive approaches.

## Traceability Approach

When drafting each AMP section, include a brief ISO 55001 alignment note (either in-text or as a footnote) referencing the relevant clause. This demonstrates deliberate alignment without making the document feel like a compliance checklist. Example:

> *This section addresses the requirements of ISO 55001:2024 Clauses 6.2.2 and 6.2.3 by establishing measurable asset management objectives and the plans to achieve them.*
