Core Philosophy: Earn the Right

Every engagement must pass three fundamental tests before advancing. The research agent validates each one.

Real Problem?

Is there a real problem we can solve? Evidence-based validation, not assumptions.

Access to Authority?

Do we have access to authority? Can we reach the people who own the problem and budget?

Willingness to Engage?

Is there willingness to engage? Are there signals that the organisation is open to change?

Qualifying out is a valid outcome. If research reveals no real problem or no path to authority, that's valuable intelligence. Document the finding and move on.

BEAM Integration

All research output feeds directly into BEAM Stage 1 qualification. The agent structures every finding to map against the three BEAM Stage 1 gates.

BEAM Stage 1 GateResearch Must Provide
Problem Domain Identified Evidence of a real business problem that aligns with our capabilities. Quantified pain where possible, with supporting signals from news, hiring, technology, and executive commentary.
Access to Authority Mapped buying committee with identified economic buyer, technical evaluators, and potential champions. LinkedIn profiles, titles, and engagement angles for each.
Willingness to Diagnose Signals that the organisation is open to exploring solutions: active initiatives, budget allocation, recent changes, event participation, or public statements about the problem domain.

Targeted Discovery

When a specific target company is provided, the agent conducts a focused discovery interview across four categories to gather context before beginning research.

Your Business & Problem You Solve

  • What does your company do in one sentence?
  • What specific problem do you solve for customers?
  • What is your unique value proposition?
  • Who is your ideal buyer persona (title, department)?
  • What industry verticals do you focus on?

Engagement Context

  • Why this company specifically?
  • Have you had any prior contact or relationship?
  • Is there a triggering event (news, funding, leadership change)?
  • What is your desired outcome from this engagement?
  • What is your timeline for engagement?

Qualification Criteria

  • What is the minimum deal size that makes this worthwhile?
  • Are there any disqualifying factors (competitors, contracts)?
  • What does success look like in 90 days?

Output Preferences

  • Do you want a full dossier or a quick assessment?
  • Should outreach templates be included?
  • What format do you prefer (HTML, Markdown, both)?

Pipeline Mode Discovery

When no specific target is provided, the agent runs an expanded discovery to build a pipeline of prospects from scratch.

Your Business

  • What does your company do?
  • What problems do you solve?
  • What is your unique differentiation?

Target Market

  • What industries do you serve?
  • What company size range (revenue, employees)?
  • What geographies are in scope?

Ideal Customer Profile

  • Describe your best existing customer
  • What made them a great fit?
  • What patterns do your best deals share?

Engagement Goals

Existing Intelligence

  • Any warm introductions or referral paths?

Output Preferences

  • Individual dossiers or pipeline summary?
  • Depth of research per prospect?
  • Preferred output format?

Phase 1: Define Problem Domain

Before any company research begins, define what you are looking for. This prevents aimless research and ensures every finding maps back to a business problem.

  • Articulate the problem hypothesis — State the specific business problem you believe the target may have, based on your discovery interview and market knowledge.
  • Define problem signals — List the observable indicators that would confirm the problem exists: hiring patterns, technology choices, news events, financial indicators.
  • Define disqualifying signals — List the indicators that would rule out the problem: recent solution purchase, competitor lock-in, no budget authority.
  • Set research scope — Define boundaries: time range for news, depth of competitor analysis, number of contacts to identify.

Phase 2: Problem Signal Identification

Systematically search for evidence that the problem exists at the target organisation. Six signal categories to investigate.

Pain Signals in News

  • Press releases mentioning challenges or strategic shifts
  • Industry analyst reports referencing the company
  • Earnings calls or investor presentations discussing pain points

Job Postings

  • Roles that indicate capability gaps
  • New department or team formations
  • Senior hires suggesting strategic pivots

Technology Gaps

  • Legacy technology stack indicators
  • Missing capabilities compared to peers
  • Integration challenges visible in job postings or forums

Strategic Priorities

  • Annual report themes and stated goals
  • Board-level initiatives or transformation programs
  • M&A activity suggesting capability acquisition

Industry Benchmarks

  • Performance gaps vs industry peers
  • Regulatory compliance requirements
  • Market share trends

Executive Commentary

  • Conference presentations and panel discussions
  • LinkedIn posts and articles from leadership
  • Podcast or media interviews

Be honest: If you don't find evidence of the problem, document that. Absence of signals is itself a signal. Do not manufacture evidence to justify engagement.

BEAM Buying Roles

Map each identified contact to their BEAM buying role. Understanding who plays which role is critical for engagement strategy.

BEAM RoleDescriptionTypical TitlesWhat They Care About
Economic Buyer Owns the budget and makes the final purchasing decision. Can say yes when everyone else says no. CEO, CFO, CIO, VP, GM, Department Head ROI, business impact, risk mitigation, strategic alignment
Technical Evaluator Assesses technical fit, integration requirements, and implementation feasibility. CTO, Solution Architect, Engineering Lead, IT Director Technical capabilities, integration, security, scalability
Champion / Sponsor Internal advocate who wants the solution to succeed. Provides inside intelligence and builds internal support. Director, Senior Manager, Project Lead, Business Analyst Solving their daily pain, career advancement, team efficiency
Gatekeeper Controls access to decision-makers. Can block progress but cannot approve deals. Procurement Manager, Executive Assistant, Legal Counsel Process compliance, vendor management, risk, contractual terms

BEAM Qualification Assessment

Score each BEAM Stage 1 gate to determine whether the prospect warrants engagement.

Gate 1: Problem Domain

RatingCriteria
STRONGMultiple confirmed signals. Quantifiable pain. Clear alignment with our capabilities.
MODERATESome signals present. Problem likely exists but not fully confirmed. Reasonable alignment.
WEAKLimited signals. Problem may exist but evidence is thin. Marginal alignment.
NONENo evidence of problem. No alignment with our capabilities. Disqualify.

Gate 2: Access to Authority

RatingCriteria
STRONGEconomic buyer identified. Direct or warm introduction path available. Multiple committee members mapped.
MODERATEKey contacts identified. No warm introduction but viable outreach path. Partial committee mapping.
WEAKLimited contacts found. No clear path to economic buyer. Cold outreach only.
NONECannot identify decision-makers. No viable access path. Disqualify.

Gate 3: Willingness to Diagnose

RatingCriteria
STRONGActive initiative underway. Budget allocated. Public statements about seeking solutions.
MODERATESome willingness indicators. Strategic priority identified. Budget cycle aligns.
WEAKNo clear willingness signals. May be receptive but no evidence. Speculative engagement.
NONEActive resistance or recent competitive lock-in. Not open to engagement. Disqualify.

Output Formats

The research agent produces structured deliverables designed for immediate use in BEAM Stage 1 selling activities.

Self-contained HTML prospect dossier with interactive sections, BEAM gate scoring, and buying committee visualisation.

Markdown Dossier

Structured Markdown document with all research findings, suitable for CRM import or team collaboration.

Engagement Timeline

Sequenced outreach plan with personalised templates, channel recommendations, and follow-up cadence.
