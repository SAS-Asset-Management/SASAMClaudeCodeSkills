# Section 2 — Introduction

## Purpose

Frame the engagement for a reader who has the accepted proposal beside them.
Set up the report structure and tell the reader exactly where to find each
category of content.

## Length budget

One to two pages, 500 to 900 words.

## Required elements

1. **Context paragraph.** The governing obligation, the MDR standard section
   under review, the review subject, and the independent reviewer. Name the
   accepted proposal and its date for traceability, drawn from
   `engagement.yaml` (client, code, reviewer, start).
2. **Purpose of the review.** A roadworthiness check, not an audit: surface the
   current maturity position against a structured, defensible framework and
   hand the client a prioritised improvement view the receiving authority can
   engage with.
3. **Scope.** The pack taxonomy (domains and subjects from `pack.yaml`), the
   condition assessment process streams under review, the submitted datasets,
   and the documented governance around them.
4. **What is not in scope.** Licensing, accreditation, regulatory enforcement,
   individual performance assessment, and financial audit — name each so the
   reader does not infer them.
5. **Engagement parameters.** Duration, interview count against
   `engagement.interviewCeiling`, and artefact count from the evidence
   schedule. Do not invent numbers.
6. **Structure of this report.** One sentence per section.
7. **Conventions.** Australian English; the pack scale with its level labels;
   confidence ratings Low, Medium, High; say versus do terminology and why it
   matters.

## Forbidden

- No findings — the introduction orients, it does not assess.
- No scores — scores live in Sections 4 and 5.
- No interview quotes — attribution lives in the subject sections under the
  cohort attribution convention.

## Plots

None. This section is text only.
