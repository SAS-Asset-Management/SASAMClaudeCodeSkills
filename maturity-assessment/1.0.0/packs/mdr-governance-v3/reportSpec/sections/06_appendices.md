# Section 6 — Appendices

## Purpose

Hold the evidence registers and reference material that underpin the narrative
without inflating it. Appendices are read selectively; each is separately
bookmarked in the PDF.

## Appendix inventory

| App | Title | Content | Source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| A | Framework rubrics | The pack's subject rubrics reproduced verbatim | `packs/<packId>/rubrics/` |
| B | Findings register | Every finding, status, client response, scoring impact | `findings/runNN/` |
| C | Artefact register | Every artefact reviewed — title, revision, review path, provenance | `reviews/` and `evidence/ARTEFACT_SCHEDULE.md` |
| D | Interview register | Every interview — role archetype, date, duration, subjects evidenced | `interviews/` |
| E | Improvement roadmap | All per subject recommendations consolidated by domain, no delivery horizons | Section 5 recommendations |
| F | Compliance matrix summary | Conformance distribution and severity rollup, when the matrix track ran | `compliance/` |
| G | Glossary | Acronyms and defined terms | Pack and MDR standard terminology |

## Content rules

- Every register row is a verbatim lift from source; if narrative and appendix
  disagree, the appendix is wrong and must be fixed at source.
- Appendix A is a reproduction, not a rewrite — formatting may change, content
  does not.
- Appendix E consolidates recommendations; nothing appears here that is not in
  a Section 5 subsection, and no delivery horizons, dates, or timeframes —
  actions and owners only.
- Appendix F appears only when `complianceMatrix` ran for the engagement; use
  the fixed conformance taxonomy (Complete, Partial, Not at all, TBC, Out of
  scope) and severity taxonomy (Critical, High, Medium, Low, n/a) exactly.

## Plots

None. Appendices are tabular; the closed catalogue plots live in Sections 1
and 4 only.
