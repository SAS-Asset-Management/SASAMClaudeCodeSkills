# 14 — Uncertainty

## Definition

The extent to which confidence, limitations, and data quality caveats are declared on records and datasets. Covers data quality flags, assessor confidence fields, known limitation statements, and communication of uncertainty to downstream users.

## Evidence Types

- A confidence or certainty field on records
- Data quality flag columns such as estimated, derived, or sampled
- A documented limitation or caveat statement accompanying a dataset
- Methodology notes that declare assumptions made during capture
- Remediation log showing which records are known to be provisional

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No uncertainty is declared and everything is presented as fact. |
| 1 | Caveats are mentioned in covering notes with no record level flags. |
| 2 | Some datasets carry a confidence or estimation flag but application is inconsistent. |
| 3 | All MDR submissions carry record level confidence or quality flags and methodology limitations are declared. |
| 4 | Uncertainty is quantified, such as inspection tolerance or sampling confidence interval, and flags are reviewed on cycle. |
| 5 | Uncertainty is propagated into downstream decisions and reporting and practice is benchmarked against industry. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 13_validity and 20_stakeholderConfidence — uncertainty that is not declared erodes trust when it surfaces later.
- Key artefact types: data supply plan, submitted datasets inspected for quality flag columns, supportability questionnaire templates.
- The absence of caveats is not the same as high confidence; probe whether uncertainty has been considered at all.
