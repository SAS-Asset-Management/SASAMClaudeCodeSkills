# 16 — Investment Linkage

## Definition

The extent to which condition assessment findings demonstrably drive capital and maintenance investment decisions. Covers the traceable line from condition data through prioritisation and business case into funded renewal, refurbishment, and maintenance programmes.

## Evidence Types

- Business cases that cite condition data as the justification
- Investment papers or forward programmes referencing MDR outputs
- Decision logs linking condition findings to funded actions
- Maintenance work orders triggered by condition assessment findings
- Post investment review comparing outcome to the condition driver

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | Investment decisions are made without reference to condition assessment data. |
| 1 | Condition data is referenced informally with no traceable linkage. |
| 2 | Some business cases cite condition data but the linkage is selective and narrative. |
| 3 | Every renewal and major maintenance decision has traceable condition assessment evidence documented in decision logs. |
| 4 | Portfolio level optimisation uses condition data and trade offs between assets are made on a common basis. |
| 5 | Condition driven investment is the default and outcomes are reviewed and fed back into the assessment framework. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 17_prioritisationMethod and 19_renewalAlignment — investment linkage is the downstream expression of both.
- Key artefact types: business cases, investment roadmaps, briefing packs to the receiving authority, deliverable trackers.
- A reference to condition data in a business case is not the same as the data driving the decision; probe whether the decision would have been different without the data.
