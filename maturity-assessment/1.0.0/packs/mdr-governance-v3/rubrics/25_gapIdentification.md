# 25 — Gap Identification

## Definition

The extent to which blind spots, unassessed assets, and coverage shortfalls are actively identified, tracked, and closed. Covers the register of known gaps, the triage and prioritisation of those gaps, and the governance that holds gap closure to account.

## Evidence Types

- A gap register listing unassessed or under assessed assets and subclasses
- Categorisation of gaps by cause such as scope, access, resource, or data
- Prioritisation of gaps
- Closure actions with owners and dates
- Governance reporting on gap closure progress

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | Gaps are unknown and no register exists. |
| 1 | Gaps are acknowledged informally and are not tracked. |
| 2 | A gap register exists for some asset classes and closure actions are patchy. |
| 3 | A comprehensive gap register covers all in scope assets, is prioritised, and closure is tracked to completion. |
| 4 | Gap trends are analysed, systemic causes are addressed, and new gaps are surfaced proactively. |
| 5 | Gap identification is continuous and embedded and network wide blind spots are rare and visible. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 21_assetSubclassCoverage, 22_geographicSpread, and 24_dataCurrency — gap identification is the management response to all three.
- Key artefact types: gap assessment registers, deliverable trackers, subclass gap assessments.
- A static gap register that never closes items is evidence of awareness, not of management; probe closure rate and governance.
