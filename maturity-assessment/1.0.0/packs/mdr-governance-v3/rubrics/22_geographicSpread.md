# 22 — Geographic Spread

## Definition

The spatial distribution of condition assessments across the network. Covers route kilometres, depots, and geographic regions represented in the MDR submissions, and the identification of sections or areas that are under represented.

## Evidence Types

- Spatial references on records such as coordinates, chainage, route, depot, or zone
- A map or distribution analysis of assessments across the network
- Coverage statistics per route, section, or depot
- Identification of under represented areas
- A plan to achieve representative spread

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No spatial reference exists and geographic spread is unknown. |
| 1 | Some records carry location and there is no analysis of spread. |
| 2 | Spread is analysed for some asset classes and known concentrations or gaps are unaddressed. |
| 3 | Representative geographic spread is demonstrated for every in scope asset class and gaps are closed on plan. |
| 4 | Spread is weighted by asset density and criticality and sampling is representative by design. |
| 5 | Spatial coverage is monitored continuously, integrated with the geographic information system, and visible to stakeholders. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 21_assetSubclassCoverage and 25_gapIdentification — spread operates within each subclass.
- Key artefact types: submitted datasets inspected for location columns, inspection data extracts, depot defect registers.
- A high record count does not imply good spread if records cluster around a single depot or section.
