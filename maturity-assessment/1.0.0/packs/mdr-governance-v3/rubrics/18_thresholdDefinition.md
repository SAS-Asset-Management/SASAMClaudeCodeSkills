# 18 — Threshold Definition

## Definition

The extent to which intervention, renewal, and condition action thresholds are explicitly defined per asset class with engineering justification. Covers the thresholds themselves, the basis for their selection, and their alignment with asset criticality, failure mode, and deterioration behaviour.

## Evidence Types

- Defined intervention or renewal thresholds per asset subclass
- Engineering justification or reference standard for each threshold
- Mapping between MDR condition ratings and threshold trigger points
- Review or ratification record by the engineering authority
- Evidence of threshold application driving action

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No thresholds exist and decisions are judgement calls. |
| 1 | Thresholds exist in the heads of engineers and are not written down. |
| 2 | Thresholds are documented for some asset classes with thin or missing justification. |
| 3 | Every in scope asset class has documented thresholds with engineering justification, mapped to the MDR condition scale. |
| 4 | Thresholds are reviewed against failure and intervention outcome data and tuned periodically. |
| 5 | Thresholds are risk and cost optimised and integrated with probabilistic deterioration modelling. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 17_prioritisationMethod and 19_renewalAlignment — thresholds feed prioritisation and renewal triggers.
- Key artefact types: technical maintenance plans, condition standards, condition assessment appendices.
- A threshold without engineering justification is a number, not a standard; probe the basis.
