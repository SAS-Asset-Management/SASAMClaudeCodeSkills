# 21 — Asset Subclass Coverage

## Definition

The proportion of in scope asset subclasses for which active condition assessments are being performed and submitted under the MDR standard. Measured against the defined MDR scope of asset subclasses, not against the total asset population.

## Evidence Types

- A list of in scope asset subclasses
- A status flag per subclass covering assessed, partial, and not assessed
- Evidence of active assessments for each asset subclass
- Gap statement for subclasses not yet covered
- A plan and timeline to close coverage gaps

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | Scope is undefined and coverage is unknown. |
| 1 | Scope is defined, coverage is informally understood, and substantial subclasses are not assessed. |
| 2 | Coverage is tracked against scope and material gaps remain for some subclasses. |
| 3 | Every in scope asset subclass has active MDR assessment activity and coverage is reported each cycle. |
| 4 | Coverage gaps are closed on a planned trajectory and scope is reviewed against updates to the MDR standard. |
| 5 | Complete subclass coverage is maintained continuously and scope extension is anticipated and actioned. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 25_gapIdentification and 10_completeness — coverage is about whether a subclass is assessed at all; completeness is about how thoroughly within a covered subclass.
- Key artefact types: gap assessment registers, minimum data requirement specifications, master asset lists.
- Presence of one sample record for a subclass is not coverage; probe the population covered within each subclass.
