# 19 — Renewal Alignment

## Definition

The degree of alignment between condition based recommendations arising from the assessment framework and the renewal programmes actually funded and delivered. Covers the reconciliation between the recommended and the delivered, and the visibility of variances.

## Evidence Types

- Recommended renewal list with dates and quantities
- Funded forward renewal programme
- Reconciliation showing matched, deferred, and dropped items
- Variance reports with reasons
- Governance record showing deferral decisions and accountability

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | There is no view of recommended versus delivered renewals. |
| 1 | There is informal awareness that programmes and recommendations differ. |
| 2 | Reconciliation is attempted on some asset classes and variance is not explained. |
| 3 | Every MDR recommendation is reconciled to the renewal programme and variances are documented with reasons. |
| 4 | Variance trends are analysed and systemic gaps are surfaced and escalated to governance. |
| 5 | Programme optimisation includes condition data, risk, and portfolio constraints with a closed loop back to recommendations. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 16_investmentLinkage, 17_prioritisationMethod, and 18_thresholdDefinition.
- Key artefact types: investment roadmaps, deliverable trackers, data supply plan appendices.
- A strong alignment ratio means little if the recommendations themselves were shaped to match the budget; probe the independence of the recommendation process.
