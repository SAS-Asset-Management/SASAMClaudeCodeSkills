# 06 — Assessor Competency

## Definition

The extent to which personnel performing condition assessments are formally qualified, trained, assessed, and authorised for the specific asset subclasses and inspection levels they undertake. Covers training records, competency matrices, authorisation registers, and refresher cycles tied to MDR aligned procedures.

## Evidence Types

- Named assessors with linked qualifications, tickets, or certificates
- A competency matrix mapping personnel to asset subclasses and inspection levels
- Authorisation or sign off register for assessors
- Training records with completion dates and validity periods
- Refresher, reassessment, or revalidation schedule

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No record of assessor training or authorisation exists and anyone can submit. |
| 1 | Ad hoc training records are held by individuals with no central register. |
| 2 | Sample competency evidence exists for some assessors and some asset classes but there is no network wide register. |
| 3 | A complete competency matrix is maintained centrally, every MDR submitter is authorised for the specific asset subclass, and refreshers are tracked. |
| 4 | Competency is linked to procedure revisions, calibration exercises run across assessors, and gap analysis drives targeted training. |
| 5 | Competency data feeds workforce planning and continuous improvement, and assessor capability is benchmarked externally. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 05_procedureDocumentation — competency is meaningless without a procedure to be competent in — and 09_qaProcesses, which tests assessor output.
- Key artefact types: competency matrix, training certificate register, authorisation register.
- Do not confuse generic industry tickets with assessor authorisation specific to the MDR standard; sample certificates without a population wide register cap the score at Level 2.
