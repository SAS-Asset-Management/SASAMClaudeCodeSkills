# 07 — Rating Scale Calibration

## Definition

The extent to which condition rating scales are explicitly defined, documented, calibrated across assessors, and aligned to the MDR condition scale. Covers scale definitions, worked examples, photographic standards, inter rater consistency exercises, and reconciliation between internal scales and the MDR scale.

## Evidence Types

- A defined condition rating scale with written criteria per level
- Photographic or illustrated exemplars per rating
- Cross walk or mapping between internal scales and the MDR condition scale
- Inter rater calibration exercises or consistency check results
- Calibration training records or assessor briefings

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No defined rating scale exists and ratings are free text. |
| 1 | A scale exists for some asset classes but is text only, uncalibrated, and unmapped to the MDR condition scale. |
| 2 | A scale is defined for most asset classes with partial mapping to the MDR condition scale, and no calibration exercises have been run. |
| 3 | A single calibrated scale exists per asset subclass, formally mapped to the MDR condition scale, with exemplars and periodic inter rater checks. |
| 4 | Calibration outcomes feed back into procedure revision and assessor drift is monitored statistically. |
| 5 | The rating scale is continuously improved from outcome data, failure data, and external benchmarking, and is embedded in the digital assessment tool. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 12_consistency and 13_validity — without a calibrated scale, downstream data quality dimensions degrade.
- Key artefact types: condition standard documents, minimum data requirement specifications, technical maintenance plans.
- Assessors may apply a scale consistently within a team yet still produce output that cannot be reconciled to the MDR condition scale; locally developed scales without a mapping cap the score at Level 2.
