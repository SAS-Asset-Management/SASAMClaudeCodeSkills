# 01 — Schedule Adherence

## Definition

The degree to which MDR data submissions are made on the dates nominated in the data supply plan and the associated capture frequency forecast. Covers planned submission dates, variation management, and recovery when a date is missed.

## Evidence Types

- Submission dates, deadlines, or due dates tied to the data supply plan
- Deliverable trackers or submission receipts
- Correspondence with the receiving authority varying supply dates
- Internal schedules, Gantt charts, or programmes for MDR deliverables
- Retrospective reporting of actual versus planned submission dates

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No tracking of submission dates exists and missed submissions have no visibility or recovery. |
| 1 | Submission dates are known informally, slippage is frequent, and there is no formal recovery process. |
| 2 | A tracker exists but is maintained reactively, with a mix of on time and late submissions and ad hoc variation requests to the receiving authority. |
| 3 | A submission calendar is maintained, the majority of submissions are on time, and late submissions are flagged, recovered, and logged as variations with the receiving authority. |
| 4 | On time performance is near 100 percent, communication with the receiving authority is proactive, and reminders and forecast based scheduling are automated. |
| 5 | No submissions have been missed over a rolling 12 months, scheduling is predictive, and the receiving authority recognises the organisation as a reliable early submitter. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 04_reportingFrequency — slippage may reflect either schedule or frequency issues.
- Key artefact types: deliverable tracker, data capture frequency and forecast document, variation correspondence.
- The contractual submission obligation in the MDR standard is the anchor for this subject; test actual dates against planned dates, not intent.
