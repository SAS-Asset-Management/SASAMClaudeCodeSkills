# 03 — Submission Completeness

## Definition

Whether every required field, asset class, dataset, and deliverable in the data supply plan is populated and present in MDR submissions to the receiving authority. Measures presence at the submission package level, not record quality.

## Evidence Types

- Data supply plan scope lists covering fields, subclasses, and datasets
- Submission receipts or package manifests
- Completeness reports or null field analyses
- Gap registers showing what was not supplied and why
- Correspondence with the receiving authority about missing submissions

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | Submissions are partial or missing and there is no visibility of what is absent. |
| 1 | It is known that submissions are partial but no structured gap list exists. |
| 2 | Most required fields and classes are populated and a gap list exists but is incomplete or reactive. |
| 3 | All required fields and classes are populated, completeness reports are generated per submission, and any gaps are explained and timelined. |
| 4 | Completeness is measured and reported to leadership and an active gap closure programme carries dates and owners. |
| 5 | Completeness of 100 percent is sustained and the organisation supplies more than mandated where it improves the receiving authority's insight. |

## Scoring Notes

- Key artefact types: data supply plan, gap assessment register, deliverable tracker.
- Completeness here is scope completeness (what is supplied), not record level completeness (see 10_completeness).
- A maintained gap assessment register is a strong positive signal and typically evidences Level 2 or 3.
