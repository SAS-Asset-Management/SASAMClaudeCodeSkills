# 09 — QA Processes

## Definition

The extent to which condition assessment outputs are independently verified through quality assurance activities, including sampling, re inspection, peer review, audit, and non conformance management. Covers QA checklists, sampling plans, re inspection results, and closure of findings.

## Evidence Types

- QA checklist or inspection verification form
- Sampling plan with frequency, coverage, and selection basis
- Re inspection or peer review records
- Internal audit reports on assessment activities
- Non conformance register with corrective actions and closure

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | No QA exists and assessor output is accepted as final. |
| 1 | Informal peer checks occur on request with no plan and no records. |
| 2 | QA exists for some asset classes but the sampling rate is undefined and findings are not systematically closed. |
| 3 | A documented QA plan applies across all MDR assessments with a defined sample rate and findings tracked to closure. |
| 4 | QA outcomes feed procedure and competency revision and trends are analysed across assessors. |
| 5 | QA is risk based, dynamically adjusted, and integrated with data quality monitoring and assessor development. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 05_procedureDocumentation, 06_assessorCompetency, and the Domain 3 quality subjects — QA is the mechanism that surfaces data quality defects before submission.
- Key artefact types: process maps, technical maintenance plans, inspection schedules, audit reports.
- QA activity that is not recorded cannot be scored above Level 1 regardless of what assessors report informally.
