# 04 — Reporting Frequency

## Definition

Whether MDR data is supplied at the frequency nominated in the data supply plan, such as monthly defect data, quarterly supportability, and annual network wide condition. Covers cadence and its consistency over time.

## Evidence Types

- Stated submission frequencies per dataset
- Submission logs showing actual cadence against plan
- Data capture frequency and forecast document
- Event driven or real time feed specifications
- Evidence of varied frequency by asset class or criticality

## Maturity Ratings

| Level | Rubric |
| --- | --- |
| 0 | Submissions are ad hoc with no defined frequency. |
| 1 | Frequencies are declared per dataset but inconsistently met. |
| 2 | Frequencies are mostly met with occasional slippage and no link between frequency and data value. |
| 3 | Frequencies are met consistently, the calendar is visible to stakeholders, and frequencies are matched to asset criticality and deterioration rate. |
| 4 | Frequency is exceeded where it adds value, some real time feeds exist for critical assets, and cadence is reviewed annually against outcomes. |
| 5 | Continuous or event driven feeds serve critical data, cadence is adjusted dynamically to asset risk, and refresh rates lead the industry. |

## Scoring Notes

- Cross references 01_scheduleAdherence (are dates met) and 23_assessmentFrequency (are the underlying assessments happening at the right rate).
- Key artefact types: data capture frequency and forecast document, deliverable tracker, submission log.
- Frequency mismatches, such as a monthly plan met with quarterly actuals, are strong Level 1 to 2 evidence.
