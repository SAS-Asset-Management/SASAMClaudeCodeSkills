## Content Structure

### Executive Summary (50-100 words)

**Purpose:** Hook readers with the outcome upfront. Lead with results.

**Formula:** [Who] + [Challenge] + [Outcome with numbers]

**Example:**
> A major Australian gold producer reduced unplanned equipment downtime by 23% across their processing facility by implementing condition-based maintenance with predictive analytics. The project delivered $1.4M in annual savings within 12 months.

---

### The Challenge — Situation (150-250 words)

**Purpose:** Establish the starting point and pain. The reader should recognise themselves.

**Include:**
- Context: organisation type, scale, sector
- Specific problems they faced
- Business impact of those problems
- Why previous approaches hadn't worked

**Structure:**
```
[Opening context paragraph]

**Pain Points:**
- [Specific pain point 1]
- [Specific pain point 2]
- [Business impact]
```

**Example:**
> The client operated a gold processing facility with over 200 rotating assets including crushers, mills, and conveyors. Their maintenance approach was predominantly calendar-based.
>
> **Pain Points:**
> - 15% unplanned downtime on critical crushing equipment
> - $6M annual maintenance spend with limited visibility
> - Reactive culture — fixing failures rather than preventing them

---

### The Approach — Task & Action (200-300 words)

**Purpose:** Show HOW the problem was solved. This is the transferable value.

**Include:**
- Overall methodology
- Key phases with timeline
- Specific activities in each phase
- What made SAS-AM's approach different

**Structure:**
```
[Opening methodology paragraph]

**Phase 1: [Phase Name] ([Duration])**
[Key activities and outputs]

**Phase 2: [Phase Name] ([Duration])**
[Key activities and outputs]

**Phase 3: [Phase Name] ([Duration])**
[Key activities and outputs]

**Key Differentiator:** [What made this approach unique or effective]
```

**Example:**
> SAS-AM partnered with the maintenance and reliability teams over 8 months.
>
> **Phase 1: Critical Asset Identification (2 months)**
> Mapped all assets by criticality using risk-based methodology. Identified 35 critical rotating assets for initial focus.
>
> **Key Differentiator:** The maintenance team was involved from day one.

---

### The Outcomes — Result (150-250 words)

**Purpose:** Prove the impact with specific, measurable results.

**Must include:**
- Quantified outcomes (%, $, time)
- Before/after comparison
- Client testimonial (if available and approved)

**Structure:**
```
| Metric | Before | After | Improvement |
|--------|--------|-------|-------------|
| [Metric 1] | [Value] | [Value] | [% or $ change] |
| [Metric 2] | [Value] | [Value] | [% or $ change] |
| [Metric 3] | [Value] | [Value] | [% or $ change] |

> "[Client quote]" — [Name], [Title]
```

**Rules:**
- Never fabricate numbers — all must come from interview
- If no exact numbers, use directional language ("reduced by approximately...")
- Skip testimonial section if no approved quote available

---

### Key Learnings (100-150 words)

**Purpose:** Provide transferable insights. What can others learn?

**Include:**
- 3-4 specific, actionable lessons
- Insights that apply beyond this specific project
- Surprises or counter-intuitive findings

**Structure:**
```
1. **[Lesson title]** — [Brief explanation]
2. **[Lesson title]** — [Brief explanation]
3. **[Lesson title]** — [Brief explanation]
```

**Example:**
> 1. **Start narrow** — 35 critical assets, not 200+ total assets
> 2. **Use existing sensors** — no major capital investment required
> 3. **Involve the team** — buy-in comes from participation, not presentations

---

### Ready to Achieve Similar Results? (50-75 words)

**Purpose:** Clear call to action.

**Options:**
- Contact for assessment
- Book a consultation
- Download related resource
- Explore related services

**Example:**
> Contact SAS-AM for a practical assessment of your predictive maintenance opportunity. We start with what you have and build from there.

## Anonymisation Guidelines

When client cannot be named:

**Do:**
- "A major water utility in Victoria"
- "A leading mining company"
- "A regional local government organisation"

**Don't:**
- Leave completely vague ("A company")
- Include details that could identify (specific location + specific project = identifiable)

---

## Testimonial Rules

1. **Never fabricate quotes** — only use actual client quotes
2. **Get approval** — quote must be approved by client
3. **Attribution required** — name, title, and ideally organisation
4. **Skip if none** — better to have no quote than a fake one
