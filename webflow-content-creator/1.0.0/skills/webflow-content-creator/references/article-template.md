## Content Structure

### The Hook (50-100 words)

**Purpose:** Capture attention immediately. This is what appears in search results and social previews.

**Techniques:**
- Ask a provocative question
- State a surprising statistic
- Name a common frustration
- Challenge a widely-held belief

**Example:**
> Most AI projects in asset management don't fail because of bad algorithms. They fail because of data.

---

### The Context (100-200 words)

**Purpose:** Establish why this matters now and connect to the reader's current challenges.

**Include:**
- Industry trend or change driving relevance
- Connection to reader's likely situation
- Preview of value they'll receive

**Example:**
> After working on dozens of implementations across mining, water, and transport, we've seen the same pattern emerge. Organisations underestimate what "AI-ready data" actually means — and overestimate how close they are to having it.

---

### The Core Content (400-800 words)

**Structure options:**

**Option A: Numbered Key Points**
```
## The [Number] [Topic] [Issues/Solutions/Steps]

### 1. [Point Title]
[Explanation with evidence]

### 2. [Point Title]
[Explanation with evidence]

### 3. [Point Title]
[Explanation with evidence]
```

**Option B: Problem/Solution**
```
## The Problem
[What's broken and why]

## The Solution
[What works instead]

## How to Apply It
[Practical steps]
```

**Option C: Deep Dive**
```
## Understanding [Topic]
[Foundational explanation]

## The Technical Details
[In-depth analysis]

## Practical Application
[How to use this knowledge]
```

**Rules:**
- One idea per paragraph
- Evidence for every claim
- Examples from real experience
- Technical accuracy without jargon overload

---

### The Practical Application (150-250 words)

**Purpose:** Make the content actionable. Readers should know what to do next.

**Include:**
- Specific next steps
- Quick wins they can implement immediately
- Resources for going deeper

**Example:**
> Start small. Pick one critical asset. Clean that data. Build one model. Learn. The organisations that succeed don't wait for perfect conditions — they start with what they have and improve as they go.

---

### The Connection (50-100 words)

**Purpose:** Clear call to action that matches the content.

**Options:**
- Contact for assessment/consultation
- Download related resource
- Read related article
- Subscribe for updates

**Example:**
> Ready to assess your AI readiness? Contact SAS-AM for a practical assessment that identifies your quickest wins.
