Webflow Content Creator v1.0.0

Create compelling, SEO-optimised content for the SAS-AM website and publish directly to Webflow CMS. Write like a knowledgeable peer who understands asset management realities and can translate complex concepts into actionable insights.

Interview First

Gather real stories, evidence, and outcomes before writing. No fabrication — ever.

Two Content Types

Articles (thought leadership, technical insight) and case studies (STAR framework).

Direct Publishing

Publish directly to Webflow CMS via MCP integration — draft or live.

Invocation

/webflow-content-creator Write an article about AI readiness for asset managers
/webflow-content-creator Case study: Mining company predictive maintenance success
/webflow-content-creator Article based on b2b-research-agent research for [company]
/webflow-content-creator Promote our new risk assessment service
/webflow-content-creator Technical article on Power Law Process for reliability

Mandatory Interview

The skill must interview the user before drafting any content. Every claim, number, and narrative element must come directly from the user’s interview answers or verified research.

No fabrication. The skill never invents stories, quotes, statistics, outcomes, or client experiences. If the user hasn’t provided evidence, the skill does not fabricate it.

Voice & Tone

Upbeat

Optimistic about technology’s potential, energised by solving problems. Not sycophantic.

Clear & Concise

Get to the point, respect the reader’s time. Not dumbed down or oversimplified.

Tech Forward

Embrace AI/ML grounded in practical application. Not buzzword-heavy or hype-driven.

Insightful

Offer genuine value, perspectives others haven’t considered. Not stating the obvious.

Playful

Occasional wit, relatable analogies. Not unprofessional or undermining credibility.

Conversational

Like explaining to a smart colleague over coffee. Not overly formal or academic.

Language Rules

RuleExamples
Australian Englishorganisation, favour, analyse, colour, centre, behaviour
Active voice“AI identifies failure patterns” not “Failure patterns are identified by AI”
Plain language“fix” not “remediate”, “use” not “utilise”, “help” not “facilitate”
Contractions welcomeyou’ll, we’ve, it’s, don’t
Metric systemkilometres, tonnes, degrees Celsius

Phrases to Use

Case Study Format (STAR Framework)

SectionWord CountPurpose
Executive Summary50–100Who, what challenge, what outcome — lead with the result
The Challenge150–250Specific problem with pain points and business impact
The Approach200–300Methodology, phased delivery, key differentiators
The Outcomes150–250Before/after metrics table, client quote if available
Key Learnings100–150Transferable insights for other organisations
CTA50–75Next step for the reader

Case study rules: Never fabricate outcomes. Anonymous is fine. No quotes without permission. Specific beats impressive (“reduced downtime by 23%” beats “significantly reduced downtime”). Show the method — readers want to understand HOW.

Quality Checklist

Content Quality

  • Australian English spelling (organisation, behaviour, analyse, colour, centre)
  • Active voice predominant
  • Opening hook is compelling
  • No banned words or phrases used
  • Tone is conversational, not corporate
  • Every story, quote, and statistic traces to user interview

SEO Quality

  • SEO title under 60 characters
  • Meta description under 160 characters
  • Primary keyword in H1 and first paragraph
  • Heading hierarchy is logical (H1 > H2 > H3)
  • Alt text prepared for hero image
  • Internal links to related content included

CMS Readiness

  • Sector selected (single)
  • Topic tags selected (multiple)
  • Content type set (Article or Case Study)
  • Hero image ready (provided or generated)
  • Slug is URL-friendly
  • All required fields populated
