## 1. Comminution (Crushing & Grinding) <a name="comminution"></a>

### Primary Crusher (Jaw/Gyratory)
**Functions**: Reduce ROM (run-of-mine) material to specified product size; accept feed at
specified rate (tph); contain material and dust
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Jaw plate / mantle wear (beyond specification)
- Toggle plate failure
- Eccentric bearing failure
- Main shaft failure / cracking
- Jaw die bolt failure
- Spider cap / rim liner wear
- Hydraulic adjustment system failure
- Tramp iron damage to crusher internals
- Blockage (bridging at feed opening)

**Common Causes**: Abrasive material, tramp metal, oversize feed, insufficient lubrication,
misalignment, overloading, material properties variation, inadequate clearing of bridged material

### Secondary/Tertiary Crusher (Cone/Impact)
**Functions**: Reduce material to secondary product specification; provide consistent product
gradation
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Bowl liner / mantle wear
- Concave liner cracking
- Hydroset system failure (cone crushers)
- Countershaft bearing failure
- V-belt / drive failure
- Tramp release mechanism failure
- Crushing chamber packing

**Common Causes**: Abrasion, overloading, tramp metal, feed distribution issues, lubrication
failure, material segregation, crusher feed arrangement

### SAG/AG Mill
**Functions**: Grind material from crusher product to target P80; operate at specified throughput
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Shell liner failure (cracking, displacement, wear-through)
- Grate plate blockage or failure
- Discharge trunnion liner wear
- Mill bearing failure (trunnion bearings)
- Gearbox failure (ring gear/pinion)
- Motor failure
- Mill shell cracking at feed/discharge end
- Ball charge issues (insufficient grinding media)

**Common Causes**: Impact and abrasion wear, fatigue, bolt failure, liner material selection,
ore hardness variability, overloading, inadequate lubrication, gearbox misalignment, foundation
settlement

### Ball Mill
**Functions**: Fine grind material to target particle size; operate in closed circuit with classifier
**Common Failure Modes**: Similar to SAG mill plus:
- Discharge grate pegging
- Rubber liner tearing at bolt holes
- Inlet/outlet chute wear
- Classifier interaction issues (circulating load)

## 2. Screening & Classification <a name="screening"></a>

### Vibrating Screen
**Functions**: Separate material by size at specified cut point; remove undersize/oversize;
dewater (wet screening)
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Screen media failure (tearing, pegging, blinding)
- Vibrator bearing failure
- Vibrator shaft failure
- Spring mount failure
- Side plate cracking
- Cross member fatigue cracking
- Feed box wear
- Deck rubber wear/tearing

**Common Causes**: High-cycle fatigue, abrasive material, corrosion (wet screening), overloading,
unbalanced vibrator, incorrect screen media selection, wet sticky material

### Hydrocyclone
**Functions**: Classify slurry by particle size; separate fines from coarse; dewater
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Vortex finder wear
- Spigot wear (roping or excessive underflow density)
- Inlet feed pipe wear
- Cyclone body wear (ceramic or rubber lining)
- Blockage
- Roping (underflow too dense)

**Common Causes**: Abrasive slurry, incorrect operating pressure, feed density variation,
overloading, lining failure

## 3. Conveyor Systems (Mining) <a name="conveyors"></a>

Mining conveyors share failure modes with port conveyors (see ports-marine-taxonomy.md) but with
additional considerations:

**Additional Failure Modes Specific to Mining**:
- Belt rip from tramp metal or oversize
- Conveyor structure settlement/misalignment (particularly on waste dumps)
- Fire (coal conveyors — friction ignition from seized rollers)
- Overland conveyor curve alignment issues
- Tunnel/decline conveyor ventilation affecting belt condition
- Extreme temperature effects on belt rubber (both hot and cold)

**Additional Causes**:
- Blast damage to nearby conveyors
- Ground movement in mining areas
- Higher dust and moisture loads
- More aggressive material (sharp, abrasive, heavy)
- Run-of-mine (unscreened) material with tramp metal

## 4. Mobile Fleet — Haul Trucks <a name="haultrucks"></a>

### Haul Truck (Rigid Body — e.g. CAT 793, Komatsu 930E)
**Functions**: Transport material from pit to dump/crusher at specified payload; maintain haul
road speed; operate safely on grade
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Engine failure (turbocharger, injectors, head gasket, bearings)
- Transmission failure (torque converter, planetary gears)
- Final drive failure
- Tyre failure (blowout, sidewall damage, tread separation)
- Brake system failure (retarder, service brakes, park brake)
- Suspension strut failure (oil/gas leak, cylinder scoring)
- Steering system failure
- Electrical system failure (alternator, batteries, harness)
- Tray / body cracking
- Hydraulic hoist failure
- Cooling system failure (radiator, water pump, thermostats)
- Air intake / filtration system failure

**Common Causes**: Operating conditions (dust, heat, shock loading), operator practice (harsh
braking, overspeed, overloading), haul road conditions, altitude, component age/hours,
contamination, lubrication issues, tyre pressure management

## 5. Mobile Fleet — Excavators & Loaders <a name="excavators"></a>

### Hydraulic Excavator (e.g. CAT 6060, Liebherr R9800)
**Functions**: Excavate material at specified dig rate; load haul trucks; maintain dig face
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Hydraulic pump failure
- Hydraulic cylinder seal failure
- Swing bearing failure
- Boom/stick structural cracking
- Bucket tooth/lip wear or loss
- Track chain/pad wear
- Engine failure
- Hydraulic hose burst
- Electrical system failure
- Operator cabin environmental control failure

**Common Causes**: High-cycle fatigue (boom/stick), hydraulic contamination, seal wear, abrasive
ground conditions, impact loading, operator technique, undercarriage maintenance

### Wheel Loader
**Functions**: Load material into trucks/hoppers; clean up around crusher; stockpile management
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Transmission failure
- Axle failure
- Tyre damage
- Bucket wear
- Loader arm / linkage pin wear
- Hydraulic system failure
- Steering system failure

**Common Causes**: Shock loading, abrasive material, tyre puncture from mine debris, hydraulic
contamination, operator practice

## 6. Pumping Systems <a name="pumping"></a>

### Slurry Pump (Centrifugal)
**Functions**: Transport slurry at specified flow and head; maintain pipeline velocity above
settling velocity
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Impeller wear (erosion)
- Volute/casing wear
- Shaft seal failure (gland packing or mechanical seal)
- Bearing failure
- Shaft failure
- Suction blockage
- Cavitation damage
- Inlet liner wear

**Common Causes**: Abrasive slurry, incorrect operating point (off-BEP), dry running, inadequate
sealing water, bearing lubrication failure, foreign objects, pH/chemical attack

### Dewatering Pump (Submersible)
**Functions**: Remove water from pit/underground workings; maintain water levels below operating floor
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Motor winding failure (insulation breakdown)
- Impeller wear or blockage
- Cable damage
- Seal failure (water ingress to motor)
- Float switch failure
- Suction strainer blockage

**Common Causes**: Silt/sand ingress, cable mechanical damage, thermal overload, voltage
instability, corrosive water chemistry, seal wear

## 7. Dewatering & Tailings <a name="dewatering"></a>

### Thickener
**Functions**: Separate solids from liquid; produce underflow at target density; return clear
overflow water
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Rake mechanism failure (drive, torque limiter, arms)
- Feedwell blockage or wear
- Underflow pump failure
- Overflow launder blockage
- Flocculant dosing system failure
- Rake arm structural failure
- Torque overload / bogging

**Common Causes**: Feed density variation, clay/fines content, flocculant dosing issues, rake
mechanism wear, foundation settlement, overloading

### Tailings Pipeline
**Functions**: Transport tailings from plant to storage facility; maintain pipeline integrity
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Pipeline wear-through (internal erosion)
- Pipeline blockage
- Joint/coupling failure
- Burst (overpressure)
- External corrosion
- Support/anchor failure

**Common Causes**: Abrasive slurry, settlement velocity exceedance, water hammer, corrosion,
thermal expansion, ground movement

## 8. Drilling & Blasting Equipment <a name="drilling"></a>

### Blast Hole Drill (Rotary/DTH)
**Functions**: Drill blast holes to specified depth and pattern; maintain hole accuracy
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Drill bit wear/failure
- Rotary head gearbox failure
- Mast structural cracking
- Compressor failure
- Hydraulic system failure
- Dust collection system failure
- Pipe/rod failure (breakage, thread damage)

**Common Causes**: Ground conditions (hardness, abrasiveness), vibration, fatigue, hydraulic
contamination, overloading, operator technique

## 9. Processing Plant Equipment <a name="processing"></a>

### Flotation Cell
**Functions**: Separate valuable mineral from gangue by froth flotation; achieve target recovery
and grade
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Impeller/stator wear
- Cell body wear/corrosion
- Air blower failure
- Launder blockage or wear
- Level control failure
- Reagent dosing failure

**Common Causes**: Abrasive/corrosive slurry, chemical attack, mechanical wear, instrument failure

### Leach Tank / Reactor
**Functions**: Dissolve target mineral via chemical reaction; maintain temperature and pH
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Tank lining failure (rubber, brick, polymer)
- Agitator failure (gearbox, shaft, impeller)
- Heating/cooling system failure
- Instrument failure (pH, temperature, level)
- Overflow or spill

**Common Causes**: Chemical attack, abrasion, thermal cycling, agitator fatigue, instrument fouling

## 10. Fixed Plant Drives & Power <a name="drives"></a>

### High Voltage Motor (>1kV)
**Functions**: Drive crusher, mill, or large pump at specified speed and torque
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Stator winding insulation failure
- Rotor bar cracking (squirrel cage)
- Bearing failure
- Cooling system failure
- Terminal box failure
- Vibration-induced damage

**Common Causes**: Thermal ageing, voltage transients, contamination (dust, moisture), bearing
lubrication, misalignment, overloading, start-stop cycling

### Gearbox (Industrial)
**Functions**: Transmit torque; provide speed reduction; support shaft loads
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Gear tooth failure (pitting, spalling, breakage)
- Bearing failure
- Shaft seal failure (oil leak)
- Oil degradation
- Housing crack
- Coupling failure

**Common Causes**: Overloading, misalignment, lubrication issues, contamination, fatigue, thermal

### VSD / VFD (Variable Speed Drive)
**Functions**: Control motor speed; provide soft start; energy optimisation
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Power module failure (IGBT/thyristor)
- DC bus capacitor failure
- Control board failure
- Cooling fan failure
- Input/output filter failure
- Harmonic distortion issues

**Common Causes**: Thermal stress, power quality issues, dust contamination, age, voltage transients

## 11. Structural & Civil <a name="structural"></a>

### Plant Structures (Steel)
**Functions**: Support equipment; provide access; resist environmental and seismic loads
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Corrosion section loss
- Fatigue cracking at connections
- Foundation settlement
- Bolt/connection loosening from vibration
- Impact damage
- Overloading from material buildup

**Common Causes**: Corrosive environment, cyclic loading from machinery, inadequate coating
maintenance, ground conditions, spillage/buildup

### Tailings Storage Facility (TSF) Wall
**Functions**: Contain tailings; manage water; maintain structural stability
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Seepage through embankment
- Piping (internal erosion)
- Slope instability
- Overtopping
- Foundation failure
- Liquefaction (seismic)

**Common Causes**: Design deficiency, construction defects, water balance management, seismic
events, extreme rainfall, inadequate monitoring, progressive deterioration

## Usage Notes

- Mining equipment operates under extreme conditions — assume higher occurrence rates than
  manufacturer MTBF unless local data says otherwise.
- Production cost per hour varies enormously by operation ($10k to $500k+/hr). Quantify for
  severity assessment.
- Mobile fleet availability is typically the binding constraint — haul truck and excavator failures
  have outsized impact.
- Tailings storage failures are catastrophic — always rate TSF-related failure modes with
  highest severity where credible consequences include dam breach.
- Many mining operations are remote — spares availability and repair time significantly affect
  consequence severity.
