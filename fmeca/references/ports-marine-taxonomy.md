## 1. Ship Loading/Unloading Equipment <a name="shiploading"></a>

### Shiploader (Travelling/Radial)
**Functions**: Load bulk material onto vessels at specified rate (tph); traverse berth face to
access all holds; maintain material quality during loading
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Boom slew drive failure (motor, gearbox, slewing bearing)
- Travel drive failure
- Boom luffing failure (unable to raise/lower)
- Chute blockage or spillage
- Trimming spout malfunction
- Structural fatigue cracking (boom, portal, tower)
- Surge bin overflow or underflow
- Dust suppression system failure

**Common Causes**: Fatigue from cyclic loading, corrosion (marine environment), bearing failure,
gearbox wear, hydraulic system failure, material buildup/blockage, structural joint fatigue,
overloading, wind loads exceeding design

### Ship Unloader (Grab/Continuous)
**Functions**: Unload bulk material from vessels at specified rate; discharge to receiving hopper/conveyor
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Grab mechanism failure (open/close)
- Hoist rope wear or failure
- Trolley traverse failure
- Hopper blockage
- Structural fatigue (boom, gantry)
- Operator visibility obstruction
- Weigh system inaccuracy

**Common Causes**: Rope wear, sheave wear, grab jaw wear, hydraulic failures, corrosion, fatigue,
material properties (moisture, stickiness), overloading

## 2. Conveyor Systems <a name="conveyors"></a>

### Belt Conveyor
**Functions**: Transport bulk material at specified rate and distance; maintain belt alignment;
contain material without spillage
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Belt mistracking (running off centre)
- Belt splice failure
- Belt rip or tear (longitudinal or transverse)
- Belt surface wear (carry side or return side)
- Drive motor failure
- Gearbox failure
- Drive pulley lagging failure
- Idler bearing failure / seizure
- Idler roller shell wear
- Skirt seal wear (spillage)
- Belt slip on drive pulley
- Chute blockage at transfer points
- Take-up system failure (gravity or hydraulic)
- Belt cleaner failure (carry-back)

**Common Causes**: Material entrapment, impact damage at loading points, pulley misalignment,
incorrect tension, lagging wear, bearing lubrication failure, idler frame distortion, abrasive
material, moisture, overloading, inadequate belt cleaner maintenance, structural settling

### Stacker/Reclaimer
**Functions**: Stack material onto stockpile in controlled layers; reclaim material from stockpile
at specified rate
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Slew bearing failure
- Boom conveyor failure
- Bucket wheel failure (reclaimer)
- Travel rail alignment issues
- Luffing mechanism failure
- Cable reel failure
- Anti-collision system failure

**Common Causes**: Bearing wear, fatigue, rail settlement, corrosion, material buildup, electrical
failures, limit switch failures

## 3. Crane Systems <a name="cranes"></a>

### Container Crane (STS — Ship-to-Shore)
**Functions**: Load/unload containers from vessel; traverse along berth; position containers
precisely onto truck/AGV
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Hoist system failure (motor, brake, rope, drum)
- Trolley drive failure
- Gantry travel drive failure
- Spreader mechanism failure (twist lock, telescoping)
- Boom hinge failure
- Anti-sway system malfunction
- Conductor bar / cable reel failure
- Structural fatigue cracking (waterside leg, portal beam)
- Wind speed sensor failure

**Common Causes**: High-cycle fatigue, rope wear, brake lining wear, spreader twist lock wear,
electrical failures, corrosion, wind/seismic loading, overloading, collision

### Mobile Harbour Crane
**Functions**: Lift and place cargo; provide multi-purpose ship-to-shore capability
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Hydraulic system failure (pumps, valves, cylinders, hoses)
- Slew ring bearing failure
- Boom cylinder failure
- Outrigger mechanism failure
- Overload protection system failure
- Engine/power unit failure

**Common Causes**: Hydraulic contamination, seal wear, bearing wear, fatigue, overloading,
engine maintenance deficiency, corrosion

### RTG / Straddle Carrier
**Functions**: Stack and retrieve containers in yard; transport containers between quay and yard
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Hoist mechanism failure
- Steering system failure
- Engine/drive failure
- Spreader malfunction
- Tyre failure
- Proximity/positioning system failure

**Common Causes**: High utilisation wear, hydraulic failures, engine maintenance, tyre damage
from debris, electrical system failures, software faults

## 4. Wharf & Berth Structure <a name="wharf"></a>

### Wharf Deck
**Functions**: Support operational loads (cranes, cargo, vehicles); provide safe working platform;
resist environmental loads (wave, current, seismic)
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Concrete spalling (reinforcement corrosion)
- Deck joint failure
- Surface wear/rutting
- Drainage system blockage
- Settlement/displacement
- Expansion joint failure

**Common Causes**: Chloride-induced reinforcement corrosion, carbonation, overloading, impact
damage, poor drainage, ground movement, age

### Piles & Substructure
**Functions**: Transfer structural loads to seabed; resist lateral loads (berthing, mooring, seismic)
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Steel pile corrosion (splash zone, tidal zone, mudline)
- Concrete pile deterioration
- Timber pile marine borer damage
- Pile-cap connection degradation
- Scour around pile base
- Cathodic protection system failure

**Common Causes**: Marine corrosion, chloride attack, marine borers (timber), wave action/scour,
impact from vessels, inadequate cathodic protection maintenance, age

### Fender System
**Functions**: Absorb vessel berthing energy; protect wharf structure; guide vessel alongside
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Fender rubber deterioration/cracking
- Fender panel damage/loss
- Chain/anchor failure
- Fender unit displacement
- Reduced energy absorption capacity

**Common Causes**: UV degradation, vessel impact exceeding design, fatigue, chain corrosion,
bolt failure, vandalism

### Mooring Equipment
**Functions**: Secure vessel alongside berth; resist wind, current, and wave forces
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Bollard foundation failure
- Quick-release hook failure
- Mooring line guide wear
- Capstan/winch failure

**Common Causes**: Overloading (vessel surge), corrosion, fatigue, mechanical wear, lack of
maintenance

## 5. Navigation Aids & Marine Systems <a name="navigation"></a>

### Navigation Lights & Marks
**Functions**: Guide vessels safely through channel; indicate berth positions; comply with
maritime regulations
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Light source failure (LED/lamp)
- Power supply failure
- Daymark colour fading
- Structure tilting or displacement
- Solar panel degradation (solar-powered aids)

**Common Causes**: Age, storm damage, corrosion, battery failure, bird nesting, collision

### Dredging & Channel Maintenance
**Functions**: Maintain declared channel depth; manage siltation
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Declared depth not maintained (siltation exceeds dredge rate)
- Survey equipment failure
- Dredge plant breakdown

**Common Causes**: Siltation rates, storm events, dredge maintenance, budget constraints

## 6. Bulk Material Handling <a name="bulkhandling"></a>

### Hopper & Feeder
**Functions**: Receive material; regulate feed rate to downstream conveyor; contain dust
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Hopper blockage (arching, ratholing)
- Feeder drive failure
- Liner wear (abrasion, impact)
- Grizzly/screen blockage
- Level instrument failure
- Dust extraction failure

**Common Causes**: Material properties (moisture, cohesion, particle size), liner wear, drive
component failure, instrument contamination, air system failure

### Dust Suppression / Extraction
**Functions**: Control airborne dust to meet regulatory limits; protect equipment and personnel
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Spray nozzle blockage
- Pump failure
- Filter/baghouse blockage
- Fan motor failure
- Water supply failure
- Dust monitoring system failure

**Common Causes**: Water quality (scale, sediment), pump wear, filter overloading, ducting leaks,
nozzle wear, instrumentation failure

## 7. Port Services & Utilities <a name="portservices"></a>

### Shore Power / Electrical Distribution
**Functions**: Supply power to port operations; provide shore power to vessels (where fitted)
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Transformer failure
- Switchgear failure
- Cable fault (insulation failure, joint failure)
- Emergency generator failure
- UPS failure

**Common Causes**: Insulation degradation, overloading, corrosion (marine atmosphere), cable
damage, age, inadequate maintenance

### Port Lighting
**Functions**: Illuminate operational areas to specified lux levels; provide security lighting
**Common Failure Modes**:
- Light fitting failure
- Mast/pole structural failure
- Control system failure (timers, photocells)
- Cable fault

**Common Causes**: Lamp/LED end of life, corrosion, vibration, electrical faults, storm damage

## Usage Notes

- Port equipment operates in aggressive marine environments — corrosion is a dominant failure
  mechanism across almost all equipment classes. Assume accelerated degradation rates compared
  to inland equivalents.
- Many port assets are high-consequence single-point-of-failure items (e.g. a single shiploader
  serving one product). Factor this into severity assessment.
- Vessel delay costs are a key severity driver — quantify in $/day where possible.
- Tidal and wave loading add fatigue cycles that are often underestimated in structural FMEAs.
- Coal, iron ore, and grain terminals each have specific material handling challenges — adapt
  failure modes to the commodity being handled.
