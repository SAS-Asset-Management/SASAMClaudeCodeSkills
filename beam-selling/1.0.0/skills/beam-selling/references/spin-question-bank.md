# marcov.BEAM — SPIN Question Bank

A comprehensive bank of SPIN questions organised by stage and topic area. Select and adapt questions relevant to your specific engagement.

---

## SPIN Overview

| Type | Purpose | When to Use | Example |
|------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **S**ituation | Understand the prospect's current context | Early stages; use sparingly — do your homework first | "How is your team currently handling [process]?" |
| **P**roblem | Uncover difficulties, dissatisfaction, and challenges | Stage 2 primarily; surface real pain | "What challenges are you facing with [area]?" |
| **I**mplication | Develop the severity and urgency of problems | Stages 2–3; make the pain felt | "What impact does that have on [outcome]?" |
| **N**eed-payoff | Get the prospect to articulate the value of a solution | Stages 3–4; let them sell themselves | "How would it help if you could [capability]?" |

## Stage 1: Qualify — Situation Questions

Use these to confirm basic context. Keep it brief — most situation data should come from your b2b-research-agent dossier.

### Company Context
- "Can you give me a quick overview of how [department/function] operates today?"
- "What's your team structure for [area]?"
- "How long have you been in your current role?"
- "What are the top priorities for your team this year?"

### Current State
- "What systems or tools are you currently using for [area]?"
- "How was the current approach put in place? When?"
- "Who are the key stakeholders involved in [area]?"
- "What does your budget cycle look like for [area]?"

### Trigger Exploration
- "What prompted you to take this meeting / look into this?"
- "Has anything changed recently that's made this more of a priority?"
- "Is there a specific event or deadline driving this?"

---

## Stage 2: Diagnose — Problem and Implication Questions

This is where deals are won or lost. Spend the most time here.

### Problem Questions — General

- "What are the biggest challenges you're facing with [area]?"
- "How satisfied are you with your current approach to [process]?"
- "What frustrations do you hear from your team about [system/process]?"
- "Where do you see the biggest gaps or risks in [area]?"
- "What happens when [current process] breaks down or fails?"
- "How often does [problem] occur? Is it getting worse?"
- "What have you tried so far to address [problem]?"
- "Why hasn't that worked?"

### Problem Questions — Technology
- "What limitations are you hitting with your current [system]?"
- "How well does [current tool] integrate with the rest of your tech stack?"
- "Are there manual workarounds your team has built around [system]?"
- "What data or visibility are you missing today?"

### Problem Questions — Operations
- "Where do delays or bottlenecks typically occur in [process]?"
- "How do you currently handle [specific operational scenario]?"
- "What's the error or rework rate in [area]?"
- "How do you manage [compliance/regulatory] requirements today?"

### Problem Questions — People and Organisation
- "How hard is it to find and retain people with the skills you need for [area]?"
- "How much time does your team spend on [low-value activity]?"
- "What's the impact on morale when [problem] keeps happening?"

### Implication Questions — Financial
- "What does [problem] cost you annually — in direct costs?"
- "What's the lost revenue or missed opportunity from [problem]?"
- "How does [problem] affect your ability to hit your financial targets?"
- "If we put a dollar figure on the time wasted on [workaround], what would that be?"

### Implication Questions — Operational
- "What impact does [problem] have on [downstream process]?"
- "How does [problem] affect your delivery timelines?"
- "What's the ripple effect when [failure scenario] occurs?"
- "How does this affect your ability to scale?"

### Implication Questions — Strategic
- "How does [problem] affect your ability to achieve [strategic goal]?"
- "What does your board/leadership think about [problem area]?"
- "How does this compare to what best-in-class organisations are doing?"
- "If this continues for another 12 months, what's the likely outcome?"

### Implication Questions — Risk
- "What's the compliance or regulatory risk if [problem] isn't addressed?"
- "Have there been any incidents or near-misses related to [problem]?"
- "What happens to your reputation if [failure scenario] occurs publicly?"
- "How does [problem] affect your audit or governance posture?"

### Implication Questions — Competitive
- "How does [problem] affect your competitive position?"
- "Are your competitors ahead of you in [area]? What's the gap?"
- "What market opportunities are you missing because of [limitation]?"

## Stage 3: Align — Implication and Need-Payoff Questions

Transition from diagnosing problems to building the vision of a solution.

### Implication Reinforcement
- "You mentioned [problem] — how is that connected to [broader strategic goal]?"
- "We've identified [X problems] — which one keeps you up at night?"
- "If you had to prioritise, which of these issues has the biggest impact on [KPI]?"
- "Who else in the organisation feels the pain from [problem]?"

### Need-Payoff Questions — Capability
- "How would it help if you could [specific capability]?"
- "What would it mean for your team if [problem] were resolved?"
- "If you had real-time visibility into [area], how would that change your approach?"
- "Imagine you could [desired outcome] — what would you do differently?"

### Need-Payoff Questions — Value
- "What value would you place on being able to [outcome]?"
- "If we could reduce [metric] by [X%], what would that be worth?"
- "How would [capability] help you achieve [strategic priority]?"
- "What would success look like for you in [timeframe]?"

### Need-Payoff Questions — Organisational Impact
- "How would solving [problem] affect [other department/team]?"
- "If this were fixed, how would that change the conversation with your board?"
- "What would your team be able to focus on if they weren't dealing with [problem]?"
- "How would [outcome] affect your ability to attract and retain talent?"

### Need-Payoff Questions — Decision
- "What would need to be true for you to move forward with a solution?"
- "What criteria will you use to evaluate potential approaches?"
- "Who else would need to be convinced that this is worth pursuing?"
- "What's the ideal timeline for having this resolved?"

---

## Stage 4: Propose — Need-Payoff Questions

Reinforce the value of your specific recommendation.

### Solution Validation
- "Based on what we've discussed, does [proposed approach] address your key concerns?"
- "Is there anything we've missed that's important to you?"
- "How does this compare to other approaches you've considered?"
- "What questions or concerns do you have about the proposed approach?"

### Value Confirmation
- "Does the expected [outcome] justify the investment we've outlined?"
- "How does this compare to the cost of continuing as-is?"
- "What ROI would your leadership need to see to approve this?"

### Decision Process
- "Walk me through how a decision like this typically gets approved."
- "Who else needs to review this proposal?"
- "Is there anything that could derail this decision?"
- "What's a realistic timeline for making a decision?"

---

## Stage 5: Commit — Validation Questions

Confirm alignment and clear the path to agreement.

### Final Validation
- "Are there any remaining concerns we haven't addressed?"
- "Is there anything in the terms that gives you pause?"
- "What would make you feel more confident about moving forward?"
- "Is there anyone else who needs to weigh in before we proceed?"

### Logistics
- "What does your contracting or procurement process look like?"
- "Are there any internal approvals we should be aware of?"
- "What's the preferred start date?"
- "Who should we coordinate with for the delivery kickoff?"

---

## Stage 6: Deliver & Renew — Continuous Discovery

Keep discovering new needs throughout delivery.

### Satisfaction and Value
- "How is the engagement going from your perspective?"
- "Are we meeting the success criteria we agreed on?"
- "What's working well? What could be improved?"
- "Have you seen the impact we discussed in [specific area]?"

### Expansion Discovery
- "Now that we've addressed [original problem], are there adjacent areas where you see similar challenges?"
- "What's next on your roadmap that we might be able to help with?"
- "Are there other teams or departments facing similar issues?"
- "What's changed in your priorities since we started?"

### Reference and Advocacy
- "Would you be open to sharing your experience as a reference for similar organisations?"
- "Would a case study be something your team would be comfortable with?"
- "Are there colleagues in your network who might benefit from a similar approach?"
